# Audit Findings — Full-Piece Review

**Date:** 2026-04-11
**Scope:** All 17 chapters, close diagnostic read, three biggest issues per chapter.
**Ranked by:** impact on reader experience, not formal severity.

---

## Method

Each chapter read in isolation for:
- Voice discipline (rule 1a speech imperatives, fragments, past-tense slips, interiority)
- Glitch rendering (rule 10 across-sentences placement, narrator-runs-ahead)
- Sweep/pacing (rule 3a varied speed, texture landings)
- Emotional density (rule 11 memory as monofocus efficiency)
- Consistency (list state, motif recurrence, stable anchors)

---

## Cross-cutting patterns

1. **A-stacks at emotional centres.** The fridge inventory, the photograph stack, and the room walkthroughs default to undifferentiated enumeration rather than varied-speed sweeps with landings. Rule 3a enforcement needs to extend beyond the walkthroughs.
2. **Rule-1a speech leakage in Chs 3, 8, 9, 11.** The *answer is yes / explain about / the question is* forms are distributed variants of the *say yes* pattern.
3. **Glitches announced before the body reaches them.** Chs 5, 6, 10, 11. The narrator does the discovery before the hand arrives. Reordering recovers rule 10 lift.
4. **Grocery list underweighted.** Appears in Chs 4, 13, 15 and never lands as hard as the wall phone does.
5. **Stable anchors stable but not felt.** The rectangle of light is strictly repeated across chapters but passed through in the middle chapters without the body stopping against it.
