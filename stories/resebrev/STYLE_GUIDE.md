# Style Guide

## Project Style

`Resebrev` should read like raw travel letters shaped into literature. The prose must keep momentum, comic timing and bodily immediacy while the manuscript architecture does the deeper moral work.

## Language

- Main prose in Swedish.
- Preserve English dialogue when English is the actual social language of the scene.
- Do not normalize all spelling and travel-era phrasing too early; some roughness is historical texture.
- Correct only what blocks reading, breaks meaning, or distracts from the literary surface.

## Chapter Headers

Use a consistent chapter convention in the publication layer:

```md
# 01. Bombay

_Dag 1. Bombay. 2 oktober 2003._
```

The title may be literary. The italic line preserves source orientation.

## Prose Handling

Prefer:

- strategic cuts
- scene compression
- light bridge sentences
- retained dialogue
- preserved comic turns
- sharpened paragraph rhythm

Avoid:

- explanatory moral commentary after every scene
- smoothing the narrator into present-day correctness
- expanding travel logistics unless they create dependence, risk or transition
- abstracting local people into "poverty", "culture" or "the East"

## Repetition

Repetition is useful when it becomes motif: money, bargaining, transport, hunger, service, hands, temples, scams.

Repetition is harmful when it merely repeats the same backpacker complaint.

## Tone

The tone should be alive, intelligent and uneasy.

The book may be funny. It may be beautiful. It should not become comfortable.

## Editing Priority

1. Preserve energy.
2. Clarify scene function.
3. Cut redundancy.
4. Sharpen moral ambiguity.
5. Polish language last.
