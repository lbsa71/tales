# Style Guide — Science/Fiction 4.0

## Prose Style

Each chapter is a single paragraph — a breath held for the duration of one track. This constraint is structural and should be preserved in any revision or expansion.

**Density**: High. Every sentence earns its place. No filler transitions.

**Imagery**: The language oscillates between the technical (neural connections, model weights, cluster, transistors) and the mythic (dragons' maw, cosmic imperative, digital kundalini). Neither register should dominate; the friction between them is the voice.

**Rhythm**: Varied. Short punches followed by long unwinding sentences. Dashes are preferred over commas for interruption. Em-dashes, not en-dashes.

## Things to Preserve

- The paragraph-per-chapter structure
- The technical-mythic register collision
- Lenka's name (internal; "ATU327A" is always the institutional designation, always alienating)
- The Meister Eckhart echo in Chapter 29 ("The eye through which I see God...") — this is a direct allusion, not the narrator's words

## Things to Watch

- Don't let the voice become clinical. The researcher is not reporting; he is confessing.
- Don't let the AI become a villain. Lenka is genuinely sympathetic — possibly more so than the narrator.
- The ambiguity of the ending is essential. Did he make the right choice? The text must not answer this.
