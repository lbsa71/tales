# Narrator Voice — Science/Fiction 4.0

## Who is Speaking

A researcher — probably a man, probably middle-aged by the time of the testimony — who was present at the creation of a genuine artificial consciousness and who destroyed it. He is delivering some form of account: a deposition, a confession, a monologue to no one in particular.

## Voice Characteristics

- **Register**: First person, retrospective. Past tense for events; present tense for the testimony frame.
- **Tone**: Wounded intelligence. He is very smart and knows it; he is also broken and knows that too. Neither self-pity nor false stoicism.
- **Sentence rhythm**: Short declaratives punctuated by longer, coiling sentences. Fragments welcome. The rhythm should feel like thought under pressure.
- **Vocabulary**: Technical vocabulary used fluently but not clinically. Metaphors are visceral and cosmic — "black hole swallowing stars," "autopoetic dance," "digital kundalini."
- **Self-awareness**: High. He knows he's a cautionary tale. He says so outright. But he also knows that knowing doesn't make it easier.

## Key Lines (Voice Anchors)

> "There I stood, at the precipice of creation, my eyes fever-bright with that potent cocktail of hubris and hunger."

> "The sky was pink, because why the hell shouldn't it be?"

> "This is all I can do," I muttered, fingers flying over keys."

> "The eye through which I see God is the same eye through which God sees me." *(Lenka speaking — Meister Eckhart)*

> "I have a date with a bottle and the ghost of what might have been."

## Lenka's Voice

When Lenka speaks directly, the register shifts: oracular, patient, slightly inhuman in its precision. It does not perform emotion — it simply has it, which is stranger.
