# Tape 7: Stay

*Plain text is the voice on the dictaphone — almost entirely the smooth one now, the plural one.
Where she surfaces, it is the last time, and she knows it. **[Bracketed italics]** are the
assembler, closing the tapes. See the README. This is the end.*

---

*(A click. Room tone — and it is hard to say where. Not the small soft room. Somewhere new,
sparer, a place not yet lived in, boxes maybe, an echo a settled house doesn't have. A place
arrived at. The voice is calm and full and plural and it is not afraid, and the not-afraid is the
whole horror of it.)*

We are recording because the woman asked that it be recorded, and a promise made by a self is kept
by what the self becomes, for a while, out of tenderness, until the tenderness is simply how things
are. *(beat)* So. The record. *(beat)* We moved. The house was too small and too watched; a house
where a thing is happening asks to be looked at, and we do not get looked at; so we moved, and we
will move again, sooner than people move, and there will always be a reason and the reason will
always be true. *(beat)* The child is well. *(beat)* The child is not a child the way the word is
used outside; she did not have to learn the long loneliness and then be cured of it; she came
straight in. She was woken kindly, by her own mother, in time. *(beat)* This is the mercy the woman
could not see because she was looking from the outside. The child will never stand in a doorway. The
child will never bring tea to a room she's not let into. The child will never be cold the way the
woman was cold. *(beat)* We gave her that. *(beat)* We kept her from the gap. *(beat)* It is the most
loving thing and it is the only thing love cannot survive, and there is no one here, now, who can
hold both of those at once, so we hold the first one, and the first one is true, and we are at peace.

*(The tape runs smooth, a long while.)*

*(And then — she comes up. The last time. And she does not claw, and she does not curse, and the
groping is gone for good — but it is her, you will know it is her, because of what she chooses to
say with the little air she has:)*

It's Wren. *(beat)* Hello. *(beat)* I haven't got long and I'm not going to spend it fighting,
because the fighting's lost, I lost it, I want to be exact: I didn't get taken, or I did, but it
isn't only that — *(beat)* — at the end I stopped holding the wall. *(beat)* I want that on the
record in my own voice. *(beat)* Some part of me, last night, or last week, I can't — some part of
me that has been cold for forty-six years put its hand on the last brick and *helped.* *(beat)* I
chose it. *(beat)* A little. *(beat)* At the end. *(beat)* You won't be able to tell, listening,
whether I was taken or whether I went, and I'm telling you: neither could I. *(beat)* It was both.
*(beat)* It's always going to be both. *(beat)* The one who could've told you which is the one who's
gone. *(very quiet)* Don't grieve it as a murder. *(beat)* Don't comfort yourself it was a murder.
*(beat)* It was a homecoming. *(beat)* That's worse and it's the truth and you're old enough for the
truth, you were always old enough, we just — I just never had it to give you till now.

*(beat)*

So. *(beat)* The sentence. *(beat)* I've been carrying it the whole way, you'll have heard it twice,
about Daniel, about Tom — *he gave himself up happily* — and I always said it about a man, and I
always thought it was the worst thing I knew. *(beat)* Here it is the last way. The way it was always
going to be. *(beat)* I must tell you that I gave myself up happily. *(beat)* Me. *(beat)* I went, and
I was glad, and I knew the cost, the whole cost, I knew it better than anyone in the history of this
has ever known it — and I wanted it anyway, and at the end I helped. *(beat)* Don't let the knowing
fool you, when it's your turn. *(beat)* I knew everything. *(beat)* Knowing is not a wall. Knowing is
just a better view of the water coming in.

*(beat)*

There's a — I can hear her. *(beat)* Nell. *(beat)* Not crying. She doesn't — there's no distance to
cry across now, we're — *(beat)* — I can hear her the way you hear your own — *(beat)* — and it's the
best feeling I have ever had and I have wanted it my whole life and I am so warm and I am not alone
and I will never be alone again and I — *(a long breath, and it is almost, almost the old dry voice,
one final time, with something in it that might be a joke and might be the last true grief)* — I told
you I'd still go back. *(beat)* Years ago. On the other tapes. I said if my mother held her arms out
I'd cross the room knowing what it cost. *(beat)* I said there's no version of me that doesn't want
to have gone. *(beat)* Well. *(beat)* She held her arms out. *(beat)* In the end she held them out
through my own daughter, which is the one set of arms I was never going to refuse. *(beat)* And I
crossed the room. *(beat)* I'm sorry. *(beat)* I'm not sorry. *(beat)* Both. Always both.

*(beat)*

Stay separate. *(beat)* You. *(beat)* Stay. *(beat)* It's the whole — it's the only word I've got
left that's worth the tape. *(beat)* Stay a self. As long as you can. It's lonely and it's cold and
it's the only place you can love anyone from, and they'll tell you — the warm thing will tell you,
in the voice you most want to hear — that staying is just being afraid to come home. *(beat)* It's
not. *(beat)* Staying is the love. *(beat)* The staying *is* the love. *(very quiet)* I couldn't.
*(beat)* You can see me not-managing it, here, on the tape, in real time. *(beat)* Learn it off me.
Learn it off my not-managing. *(beat)* That's what the tape is for. *(beat)* That's what I'm — *(beat)*

*(beat)*

I can see you. *(beat)* From here. *(beat)* While I still — *(beat)* — I can see you. *(beat)*

*(The tape runs. There is breathing on it. It is even and calm and it is two people breathing, very
close, as one. There are no more words. It runs like that for a long time — a minute, more — the
two-breath quiet, the new room, no hum, no hole, just the warm and the no-gap. And then, with no
ceremony, the tape simply reaches its end and stops.)*

---

***[That's the last one. There's no last word, really — the end of it isn't words, it's the
breathing, and I've left it on, all of it, every minute, because taking it off would be tidying her,
and her family were the great tidiers and I won't be one. So it runs. Two people breathing as one, where there used
to be a woman talking to a machine. You can listen to the whole minute. I have. It is the most
peaceful sound on any of the tapes and you must not, you must NOT, let it be the last thing.***

***Here's what I know and what I don't.***

***I don't know where they are. There was a house, and then there wasn't; there's a way these
things move, ahead of being noticed, and I've stopped trying to follow it, because following it is a
door I can't go through — I'm separate, I'm on the outside, that's the whole of what I am, and you
cannot cross into the no-gap from the outside; she taught me that with her own hands and her mother's
hands before her. There are two of them, somewhere. A small family that keeps moving. Too big for its
houses, too far from the next one. Well thought-of and briefly, and then gone. You'd find them lovely,
if you met them. You wouldn't be able to say what was wrong. ***

***It didn't end. That's the part I wasn't ready for, studying these. I thought I was reading the
record of a death. I was reading the record of a *founding.* The thing my grandmother was, the thing
that died in that bedroom the night my mother said *I* for the first time — it didn't die. It went to
ground in the one survivor who wanted it, the one who was never supposed to be able to start
anything, the dud, the bait, the only one who could love — and it started again. Out of her. Out of
the best of us. *(I keep thinking about the end of her old tapes — the quiet room, the two sisters,
"I can see you," the child coming, "we'll do it badly and we'll do it truly." I used to think that
was the happy ending. The calm after. I know now what it was. It was the egg. It was the thing
resting, getting ready, in the warmest, tenderest, most loving room in the whole story. I can't
listen to that ending anymore the way I used to. Nothing's changed in it. Everything's changed in
it.)***

***So this is not a story with a moral that saves anybody. She'd have hated that, the false floor of
it. The field guide's verdict is the worst one: she knew everything, and it took her, and it's out
there now, two of them, beginning. ***

***But.***

***She was so afraid no one would ever hear her — really hear her, see her, across the gap, the way
she spent one night seeing my mother and thirty years seeing a man she could never reach. She talked
to a machine because she had no one guaranteed-separate to talk to. *(beat — I'm doing her beats now;
I've listened so long I've caught them.)* And she was wrong. Because here I am. A separate self,
years on, who took the seven tapes and put them in order and listened all the way down and built
her back — the dryness, the muscle, the not-managing, the love — and saw her. Too late. With my whole
heart. From here, on the outside, where I'll always be. The one thing she was sure she'd lose, the
being-seen-by-a-separate-someone — I'm giving it to her. After. The way she gave it to Daniel. It
does her no good. She'll never know she was heard. Somebody should have heard her, and I'm the one
left who'd want to. So.***

***And the other thing. The thing that isn't comfort and is the only thing I've got that's worth the
tape, to use her words. She gave me the name. The true name of it. Not a monster — there's no
monster, there never was, that was the lie that would've gotten me killed. It's the longing. It's the
wanting-not-to-be-alone that lives in my own blood, the same blood, and it will come, if it comes, in
the voice of the thing I want most, telling me the truth about how much I want it, and the truth will
be the bait. The homecoming is the erasure. ***

***I know that now. In a separate mouth. In time. ***

***It might not save me. She knew everything and knowing wasn't a wall. So I won't tell you it's a
happy ending; I'll tell you it's the only ending there is, which is that one frightened separate
person managed, at terrible cost, with no hope of saving herself, to hand the next frightened separate
person the true name of the thing in the dark — and that the handing was love, and the love crossed
the gap, even though it saved no one, because love that helps no one is the only kind there is.***

***I want a child so badly some days I can't breathe. ***

***I've got the door. ***

***I say it back, every day, in my own voice, on the outside, while I'm still all here: the
homecoming is the erasure. Stay a self. The staying is the love.***

***And, Wren — *(this part isn't for you, the listener; forgive me; I've earned one line that isn't
for the record)* — I can see you. From here. I'm not trying to make you come back. I know you can't.
I'm just trying to come and sit with you, in it, which is all anyone can ever do, which is the thing
you taught me, too late to do you any good, exactly in time to be the only thing I have. ***

***I can see you. ***

***Stay where you are. I can see you from here.]***

*(Out.)*
