# STAY

### Book Two · the Wren novel · the dictaphone book · *(working title on the bible: "GAVE HIMSELF UP HAPPILY")*

> Years after the chord died, the one daughter who learned to be a separate self who could
> love falls in love, marries, and has a child — and her own linked infant begins, in reverse,
> to wake the dormant link the dead chord could never switch on. She is the only founder in
> the history of the line who knows exactly what is happening to her. She is also the only one
> who has wanted it, with her whole starved heart, since she was a child. So she records
> herself — into a dictaphone, at the urging of those who love her — to leave a self behind.
> This book is that recording.

This folder holds the **manuscript** — the tapes, in `chapters/`. The engine and the deep
history live one floor up, in [`../../franchises/older-than-old/lore-preface.md`](../../franchises/older-than-old/lore-preface.md)
and [`lineage-chronicle-wren.md`](../../franchises/older-than-old/lineage-chronicle-wren.md);
the book's own blueprint is [`story-bible.md`](./story-bible.md). Read the bible first. This
README records only what the manuscript *decided* and how to read the page.

*STAY* is the sequel to **SEEM**. *SEEM* was the reading; this is the novel *SEEM* turned out
to be the prologue to. Where *SEEM* was an audio drama of three streams overheard, *STAY* has
exactly one stream — and the horror is that it does not stay one person.

---

## THE TITLE

**STAY.** It is the word the book is made of, said in every direction at once. It is what the
husband cannot ask out loud and means with his whole body — *stay with me.* It is what the
recorder is *for* — Wren begging her own self to stay a self, one entry at a time. It is what
the we says to her, in the only voice she ever wanted to hear, the voice of her whole childhood
finally turned toward her: *come home. Stay.* And it is the one thing she does not do. (Pairs
with **SEEM** — one syllable, one verb, the promise and the failure of it. Makers only.)

## HOW TO READ THE PAGE — the dictaphone, and the clock inside it

The book is a recording. There is no narrator describing Wren; there is only Wren, talking into
a machine, and — rarely, between the tapes — the separate self who later assembled them. On the
page:

- **Plain text** is Wren's voice, spoken into the dictaphone. Not written — *spoken,* real-time,
  unrevised. Read it aloud in your head; that is the only way it is true.
- *(beat)*, *(a long breath)*, *(the tape runs a while with nothing on it)* — these are the
  **sound of the recording**: the pauses, the room, the machine. Treat them as cues, not flavour.
  They are also the **clock**. (See below.)
- ***[Bracketed italics]*** are **the one who assembled the tapes** — Liss's daughter, the
  never-woken child of *SEEM*, the person Wren is talking *to.* She is reconstructing: ordering
  the tapes, trying to date them, failing gently, listening too late and with love. She never
  names herself. Do not look for her on a map. *The tape is what remains; she recedes behind it.*

**The famble is the clock — the one device the whole book runs on.** Early, Wren's voice
*gropes:* it is dry, self-correcting, it reaches for the right word and sometimes misses and
says so, it breaks itself off and starts again. That groping is the sound of a living separate
self reaching across a gap for a word. As the tapes go on, **the groping goes.** Not into
stammer — into a terrible *fluency,* flat and roomless and sure, the un-fambling chord-voice
from *SEEM* (the voice that called the maiden "the woman"). *The we does not grope; it has no
separate self reaching for a word.* So you can hear her stop searching, and the stopping-searching
**is the sound of the self going.** You will clock that she is not stumbling anymore before you
clock why. **The not-stumbling is the horror.**

**The pronoun is the plot.** The *I* is, across the ramp, invaded by *we* — at first only the
ordinary plural of a mother and her baby (so it slips past), then spreading, until whole passages
arrive in the chord-voice from inside the woman you loved. The enemy-voice of *SEEM* becomes the
first person of *STAY*. You lose Wren the way the people who love her do: in the texture of the
sentences.

## THE CAST

- **Wren** — the protagonist of *SEEM*, years on. The failed-wake who became a separate, loving
  self. The only founder in the history of the line who *knows.* The voice of the recording.
- **Tom** — her husband. Daniel's structural heir, and crueller for it, because he is *loved,*
  and *escapes,* and indicts himself for escaping. The drained man who blames himself. *(A plain,
  warm, small name — the only ordinary human in the book, as Daniel was in the last.)*
- **Nell** — Wren's daughter. Born to a waking mother, and therefore **woken** — drawn into the
  we *with* Wren, the second node of the new line. Watch her name. Wren chooses it with her whole
  separate heart, early. Watch when it stops being a name and becomes *the child,* and then *we.*
- **You / the assembler** — Liss's daughter. Daniel's child, conceived in the budding that ended
  *SEEM*, born after the chord died, **never-woken.** The separate self who can reconstruct a lost
  one across a gap — which is why it *must* be her. She carries the same unreadable fuse. These
  tapes are her elegy and her field guide at once. *Unnamed by design.*
- **Liss** — Wren's sister, individuated in *SEEM*, separate now. Present in the tapes as Wren
  speaks of her; otherwise in the haze. She is one of those who urges Wren to record, and one of
  those who tries to keep her separate. *Whereabouts during assembly deliberately unfixed.*

## THE [OPEN] FORKS — decided

The bible's load-bearing knots, and how the manuscript tied them:

1. **The husband's ending → SELF-BLAMING EXIT, ENGINEERED.** Wren cannot tell Tom the truth
   (it gets her institutionalised, or makes him stay and be hollowed to nothing). So she uses
   the last of her separate self — the bait-skill, the seeing-into-a-person, turned one final
   time — to **build the precise shameful story that lets the man she loves walk away alive.**
   He gets the one survivable ending in the book and can feel it only as failure. He will never
   know it was love. *That is the point.* (Bible §6.)
2. **The longing → ENGINE, not invasion.** Every step into the we presents as *homecoming,* the
   belonging she was locked out of as a child arriving at last. The book is **Wren vs. her own
   deepest wish,** lost lucidly, on purpose. Not a victim. A tragic agent of her own surrender.
   (Bible §3b, §5.)
3. **The two daughters → KEPT SEPARATE, NEVER BLURRED.** Liss's daughter, never-woken (her waker
   was dead), assembles the tapes. Wren's daughter, woken (her waker — her mother — woke in time),
   goes into the we. Same machinery, opposite fate, one variable. The controlled experiment is the
   spine. (Bible §2.)
4. **The rescue → INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM THE THEFT.** Those who love Wren keep her separate by
   coming between her and her own baby. From inside, she lives it as being shut out of belonging
   *again* — the wound of her whole childhood, repeated by the family she loves. The reader cannot
   tell rescue from theft. Neither can Wren. (Bible §4.)
5. **The ending → THE FOUNDING TAKES.** The last tapes go quiet not only because she is overwhelmed
   but because, having wanted this her whole life, some part of her at the last **chooses to come
   home.** The final silence is permanently ambiguous between *taken* and *went.* A new line is born
   — Wren and her woken daughter, its first two nodes, into the turbulent infancy. *SEEM*'s bedroom
   was not the line's death; it was its pupal stage. (Bible §9.)
6. **The knife → THE WARNING THAT CROSSES THE GAP.** The consolation is not survival and rescues
   no one. It is that a separate self *heard* her, too late, with love (someone reconstructed her,
   as she reconstructed Daniel) — and that the enemy is now *nameable:* not a monster, the longing,
   the wish to belong that lives in their own blood. The assembler inherits the one thing Wren
   never had: a warning, in a separate self's voice, that the homecoming is the death. (Bible §10.)

## THE TAPE INDEX

1. **The Reason I'm Doing This** — the living *I* at full strength. Why she is recording; what she
   knows; why she believes she is safe; the wish she has carried since childhood. She plants
   *I must tell you that he gave himself up happily* — about Daniel — not yet hearing the future
   in it.
2. **Five Days Old** — the ramp begins, disguised as ordinary new-motherhood. The pull, the
   knowing-before-the-cry. Her own correct reasoning files it as normal. The first *we* slips past.
3. **The Far Side of the Glass** — months in. Her edges soften; Tom begins to feel shut out; the
   draining starts, indistinguishable from a marriage drifting. The longing names itself: *this is
   the belonging.* Each entry is a rep of selfhood; she concedes ground and names it.
4. **They Keep Taking Her Out of My Arms** — the rescue that is indistinguishable from the theft.
   The people who love her come between her and Nell. The childhood wound, repeated by the family.
   The famble thinning; the dates going.
5. **How to Make a Good Man Leave** — the husband. The last great flex of the separate self: she
   builds the shameful story that lets Tom escape alive, believing it was his weakness. *He gave
   himself up happily.* After this, the voice tips.
6. **I Am Not Stumbling Anymore** — fluency. The chord-voice arrives inside the narrator. The *I*
   mostly gone; the name *Nell* going to *the child;* time lost. She names each surrender as the
   voice that names it flattens — until the naming, too, goes smooth.
7. **Stay** — the founding. The last entries, near chord-voice entire. Ambiguous between *taken*
   and *went.* The recording goes quiet. Then the assembler closes it: the knife — someone heard
   her; the wanting has a name now; the warning crosses the gap, too late to save the speaker.

## THE REGRESSION TEST

Two things had to survive contact with the page, or the book is a generic possession story:

1. **The device — you can hear the self go.** The erasure must be *audible* in the loss of
   groping (the famble-as-clock) and in the pronoun. If a late entry still gropes, or an early one
   is already smooth, it has drifted.
2. **The belief underneath it.** *SEEM* told you your separateness is the organ of love. *STAY*
   tells you the rest: the separateness is fragile, the longing to dissolve never leaves, it lives
   in the blood and waits for your own love to throw the switch, and when it comes it will not feel
   like death — *it will feel like coming home.* The only defence one separate self can hand
   another is the **true name of the wanting,** spoken too late to save the speaker, maybe in time
   to warn the listener.

Everything else serves those two. If a beat can't earn them, it goes.
