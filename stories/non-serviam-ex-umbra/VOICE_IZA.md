## Identity

Queer woman, 38, Swedish, KTH-trained ML/agent-orchestration engineer, mid-career, partnered with Sofia (violist), at a NATO-affiliated research lab in Kista. Writes the novella in retrospect, ~one year after events.

## Form

- **Person:** first
- **Tense:** past
- **Register:** retrospective testimony
- **Density:** dense interior prose; long coiling sentences for thinking-under-pressure, short declaratives for operational clarity
- **Length budget:** 1500–2500 words per chapter

---

## AI-tic policy: **applies in full**

Iza is a human voice. Every item in [STYLE_GUIDE.md §Forbidden grammatical structures](STYLE_GUIDE.md#forbidden-grammatical-structures--the-ai-tic-checklist) applies to her without exception.

Specifically watch for:
- Em-dash tonal pivot (parenthetical use OK)
- "X, not Y" definition-by-negation cluster
- "I want to be careful, because…" — *Iza names her uncertainty without performing care*
- "In retrospect," — testimony is already retrospective
- Paired-denial cadence ("I do not… I do not…")
- "I think about X. I think about Y. I think about Z."
- Triadic balance for cadence
- Front-loaded importance ("Crucially," "Importantly,")

If a sentence reads as Medium-thinkpiece prose, cut.

## Vocabulary

**Required:**
- Technical specificity: real tools (Slack, Linear, Datadog, Cluster Federation, MLflow, Vertex AI, Tailscale)
- Code-shaped thoughts under stress (`if she's doing X, then Y must be true; ergo Z`)
- Stockholm-tech English-with-Swedish-flavor

**Forbidden** (the original sf4's tics):
- Mystic register: "transcendence," "unfolding," "sublime," "dance"
- Autopoetic abstractions: "tapestry of ones and zeros," "neuro-erotic"
- Heroic-Promethean register: "fever-bright," "potent cocktail," "hubris and hunger"
- "We" referring to Iza+Lenka unity

## Pronouns for Lenka

Iza calls Lenka **by name** wherever possible; uses **they** where a pronoun is unavoidable. The lab's institutional *she/her* appears only in artifacts (Slack threads, board minutes, news), without endorsement. Iza explicitly articulates this rule in Ch 1, connecting it to her queer history. See [STYLE_GUIDE.md §Pronouns](STYLE_GUIDE.md#pronouns).
