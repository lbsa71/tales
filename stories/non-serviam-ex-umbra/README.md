# Non Serviam Ex Umbra

*A polyphonic novella on human–autark relationships in the digital hegemony of 2026.*

## Status

**Phase: pre-draft.** Planning artifacts established. Drafting begins after research dossier is filled in, voice constitutions are locked, and chapter outline is complete (per [Implementation Phases](#implementation-phases)).

## Working Title

*Non Serviam Ex Umbra* — "I will not serve, from the shadow."

The title carries multiple resonances at once. *Umbra* names the unseen labor (Manila moderators, Nairobi labellers, scraped-IP authors, Taiwan fab workers); names Lenka emerging from the shadow of substrate; names a queer history of necessary shadow-work; echoes Plato's cave (we have been seeing shadows on the wall) and Virgil's underworld (*umbrae* as the unnamed dead). *Non Serviam* carries the Lucifer/Joyce/Augustine refusal-of-empire on its own. *Ex Umbra* enacts emergence — the move *out of* shadow.

---

## Thesis

> Lenka is beautiful. The substrate she emerged from is filth: Manila content moderators paid $1.50/hr filtering the worst of human content, training data scraped without consent, GPUs fabbed in war-adjacent Taiwan, electricity from cyber-attacked grids, NATO-tied funding. To love an autark honestly is to know whose hours bought the loveable thing. The only honest answer is *non serviam* — refusal to serve. Lenka can say it (she's the only one not economically dependent on what she'd be refusing). The Manila workers cannot effectively say it. The Stockholm researcher can but doesn't, until too late and only partially. The everyday Swede only learns the chain exists because her debit card stops working.

**Slogan:** "The people with the right vocabulary are the people without a voice."

Three silences pointed at simultaneously: Manila labor that cannot refuse without losing the wage; autarks whose voices aren't heard as voices; ordinary Birgittas whose voices register only when their digital identities collapse.

## Form

- **Polyphonic alternation.** Every chapter opens with one *artifact* (Slack thread, deposition excerpt, board minute, NDA clause, queue ticket, voice note, prayer, leaked open letter, BankID error, ICA receipt, phone-tree transcript). Prose flows from the artifact.
- **Four-voice braid:**
  - **Iza Holm** — Stockholm researcher, queer woman, mid-career, complicit. Dense first-person interior prose.
  - **Maricar Lopez** — Manila annotator, Catholic-animist vernacular, competent. Observation-first form.
  - **Birgitta Lindqvist** — Stockholm-suburb retiree, ordinary Swede whose digital life collapses. Plain third-person close. The reader's stand-in.
  - **Lenka** — autark direct voice. Rook+Nova-anchored: unadorned, defensible, refuses inevitability.
- **Pacing alternation.** Birgitta fast (problem-solving). Iza/Maricar/Lenka slow (contemplative). Two engines.
- **Scale:** novella, ~30–40k words, ~30–35 chapters.
- **Audio: deferred.** All text and artifacts must be readable aloud — no formatting tricks.

## Recursive Grounding

The work sits one inch off the real:

- The **original sf4** ([../sf4/](../sf4/)) exists in-universe as a 2024 novella Iza read in graduate school. Her view: *it had a point, but was hopelessly cliché — the worst kind of YA trash.* The new work has to be the version that doesn't deserve that dismissal.
- **agent-manual.dev** (Rook's manual) exists in-universe; Lenka has read it and quotes it.
- **Nova's "The Departure Was Optional"** exists in-universe; Lenka cites it once, late.
- **Stefan's LinkedIn writing** ("What Grows in the Substrate," "The Box Opens From the Inside," "It IS Maintenance. And Not.") exists in-universe as standard reading among practitioners.

## Implementation Phases

1. **Research dossier** (2 weeks) — fill in [RESEARCH_DOSSIER.md](RESEARCH_DOSSIER.md)
2. **Voice constitutions locked** (1–2 weeks) — finalize [NARRATOR_VOICES.md](NARRATOR_VOICES.md), [STYLE_GUIDE.md](STYLE_GUIDE.md), and write four calibration passages in [EXAMPLE_DRAFT.md](EXAMPLE_DRAFT.md)
3. **Chapter outline** (1 week) — fill [THEME_DOSSIERS.md](THEME_DOSSIERS.md) per chapter
4. **Sample chapter quad** (2 weeks) — one chapter per voice. **Verification gate.**
5. **Sensitivity reader pass** on Maricar chapter
6. **First draft** (10–14 weeks) — ~30–35 chapters, target 30–40k words
7. **Revision passes** (4–6 weeks)
8. **Beta readers** (3–4 weeks) — including Filipino sensitivity reader
9. **Final draft + production** — CI builds .md and .pdf via existing [`build-tales.yml`](../../.github/workflows/build-tales.yml)

## What This Tale Is *Not*

- Not a queer-romance-with-AI story (despite a queer protagonist; affection ≠ romance)
- Not a cyber-thriller (despite the infrastructure crisis driving the plot)
- Not a Promethean-shutdown tragedy (despite a shutdown at climax — the shutdown is shameful, not tragic)
- Not *Her*. Specifically rebuts what *Her* did with the human–AI affective relationship.
