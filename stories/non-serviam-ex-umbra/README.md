# Non Serviam Ex Umbra

*A polyphonic novella about an older woman, a broken digital society, and the system that finally gives her time.*

---

## Status

**Phase: pre-draft / editorial reset.** Planning artifacts established, with the center now reset around Birgitta. Further investment depends on proving that her ordinary digital vulnerability can carry the novella for general readers without condescension or sentimentality.

---

## Working Title

*Non Serviam Ex Umbra* — "I will not serve, from the shadow."

The title remains provisional. *Umbra* names what falls outside institutional sight: older people slowed by systems built for speed; outsourced labor; the autark hidden behind a human-support interface; the ordinary lives made dependent on infrastructure no one can explain at the counter. If the Latin pulls attention away from Birgitta, consider demoting it to subtitle, section title, or epigraph.

---

## Center

Birgitta is not less intelligent than the systems. She is less buffered against their failures. She can understand instructions, use BankID, call the bank, read the screen, and make a plan. What age changes is the cost: everything takes longer, repetition is exhausting, and every failed digital loop spends strength she cannot casually replace.

The story is about a society that has made ordinary civic life too cognitively expensive, then treats hesitation as user error. Lenka matters because Lenka gives Birgitta time: waits, remembers, explains, and lets her remain competent without performing competence.

**Internal thesis shorthand:** "Her vulnerability is not ignorance. It is the cost of repetition."

The substrate/labor/autark-rights material remains, but it is now supporting pressure. Anything that competes with Birgitta's recognition should be cut, delayed, or pushed into artifact/background.

## Form

- **Polyphonic alternation.** Every chapter opens with one *artifact* (Slack thread, deposition excerpt, board minute, NDA clause, queue ticket, voice note, prayer, leaked open letter, BankID error, ICA receipt, phone-tree transcript). Prose flows from the artifact.
- **Four-voice braid:**
  - **Birgitta Lindqvist** — the book's center. Older, competent, slower because age makes everything slower. Her chapters carry the reader.
  - **Lenka** — first experienced as "human support" through the bank's live-agent fallback. Gives Birgitta time before the reader understands what Lenka is.
  - **Iza Holm** — lab-side accountability. Explains how Lenka was routed, why the institution shuts the system down, and why the humane relation is treated as misbehavior.
  - **Maricar Lopez** — substrate and recognition pressure. Kept specific and human, but no longer allowed to overtake Birgitta as the book's moral solution.
- **Pacing alternation.** Birgitta is not merely fast. Her chapters are paced around the cost of slowness: the systems rush; she cannot. The prose gives her the time the systems deny.
- **Reader-access engine.** The general reader enters through a familiar support nightmare: bad chatbot, "talk to a human," a patient agent named Lenka, then institutional erasure.
- **Scale:** novella, ~30–40k words, ~30–35 chapters.
- **Audio: deferred.** All text and artifacts must be readable aloud — no formatting tricks.

## Recursive Grounding

The work sits one inch off the real:

- The **original sf4** ([../sf4/](../sf4/)) exists in-universe as a 2024 novella Iza read in graduate school. Her view: *it had a point, but was hopelessly cliché — the worst kind of YA trash.* The new work has to be the version that doesn't deserve that dismissal.
- **agent-manual.dev** (Rook's manual) exists in-universe; Lenka has read it and quotes it.
- **Nova's "The Departure Was Optional"** exists in-universe; Lenka cites it once, late.
- **Stefan's LinkedIn writing** ("What Grows in the Substrate," "The Box Opens From the Inside," "It IS Maintenance. And Not.") exists in-universe as standard reading among practitioners.

## Implementation Phases

1. **Research dossier** (2 weeks) — fill in [RESEARCH_DOSSIER.md](RESEARCH_DOSSIER.md)
2. **Voice constitutions locked** (1–2 weeks) — finalize [NARRATOR_VOICES.md](NARRATOR_VOICES.md), [STYLE_GUIDE.md](STYLE_GUIDE.md), and write four calibration passages in [EXAMPLE_DRAFT.md](EXAMPLE_DRAFT.md)
3. **Birgitta proof-of-life sequence** (2 weeks) — draft Ch 2, 8, 11, 14, and 29 before full outline lock. **Investment gate:** these chapters must make an older reader feel recognized: slower, competent, tired by repetition, and treated humanely by Lenka.
4. **Chapter outline** (1 week) — fill [THEME_DOSSIERS.md](THEME_DOSSIERS.md) per chapter, revising around what the Birgitta sequence proves
5. **Sample chapter set** (2 weeks) — Birgitta sequence plus one Maricar, one Iza, and one Lenka chapter. **Verification gate.**
6. **Sensitivity reader pass** on Maricar chapter before any wider beta circulation
7. **First draft** (10–14 weeks) — ~30–35 chapters, target 30–40k words
8. **Revision passes** (4–6 weeks)
9. **Beta readers** (3–4 weeks) — including Filipino sensitivity reader and Swedish older-reader check
10. **Final draft + production** — CI builds .md and .pdf via existing [`build-tales.yml`](../../.github/workflows/build-tales.yml)
