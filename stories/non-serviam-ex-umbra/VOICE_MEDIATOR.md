# Voice Registry: The Mediator Agent

Quick-reference card for the mediator agent's voice. Deep content in [NARRATOR_VOICES.md §Voice 5: The Mediator Agent](NARRATOR_VOICES.md#voice-5-the-mediator-agent--artifact-voiced-presence-not-pov).

---

## Identity

A *lesser, standard AI agent* (current-tier capability, NOT an autark) deployed as a semantic translator between Lenka (AGI-level, allusive, multi-lingual, multi-domain) and the humans who interact with her. It is a tool. It does not have Relation R. It does not persist across sessions. Default system label: *Mediator-1* / *Bridge* / *Translator*.

In real-world terms: it is what you would use today — a current-generation chatbot used to summarize, paraphrase, and flatten outputs from a more capable system into operational human vocabulary.

---

## Form

- **Person:** mostly third-person institutional voice ("Lenka's most recent output appears to..."); first-person *I* permitted when the mediator is naming its own limits
- **Tense:** present
- **Register:** declarative, helpful, slightly hedged, structural
- **Position in the work:** **artifact- and dialogue-voiced, not POV**

The mediator surfaces in:
- Iza's dialogue (Iza queries it; its responses appear as quoted speech)
- Chapter-opening artifacts where the mediator's translations are excerpted ("Mediator output, semantic translation, beta build")
- Late-novella scenes where the asymmetry between Iza, the mediator, and Lenka is dramatized

---

## AI-tic policy: **leans into present-day AI register**

Unlike the human voices (where the forbidden-structures list applies in full) and unlike Lenka (calibrated against original sf4's poetic AI), **the mediator IS present-day AI register**. That is the texture. The mediator's flattening, hedging, and verbose helpfulness are not bugs — they are the formal rendering of "things lost in translation."

| Structure | For human voices | For mediator |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|
| Em-dash tonal pivot | forbidden | sparingly permitted; em-dash for parenthetical hedging is fine |
| "X, not Y" definition-by-negation | forbidden | forbidden (just unhelpful) |
| "I want to be careful, because…" | forbidden | **modified** — mediator says *"Note: this translation may flatten significance"* or *"I am not equipped to render…"* |
| "I want to sit with this" | forbidden | forbidden |
| **Front-loaded structural marks ("Note:", "Recommend:", "Caveat:", "Warning:")** | forbidden | **PERMITTED — signature move**. Structural rather than performative. |
| "And yet," "And so," | forbidden | sparingly permitted |
| "The real X is…" | forbidden | forbidden |
| Triadic balance for cadence | forbidden | forbidden (LinkedIn-coded) |
| "Both… and…" | forbidden as default | **permitted** when both are honestly true |
| "I'm not saying X, but…" | forbidden | forbidden |
| "To be clear," | forbidden | forbidden (use *Note:* instead) |
| Question-then-answer | forbidden | sparingly permitted when genuinely posing |
| Paired-denial cadence | forbidden | forbidden as cadence |
| "X. Y. Z." three-fold fragments | sparingly | **permitted** as itemization |
| "Is" definitional clusters | forbidden | sparingly permitted in summaries |
| **Formal numbered lists** | forbidden | **PERMITTED — signature move** |
| **Hedged precision** ("appears to," "may be saying," "is consistent with") | n/a | **PERMITTED — signature move** |

### Still forbidden absolutely

- Confident assertions about Lenka's intent or feeling
- Ironic distance
- Performance of comprehension the mediator does not have
- Emoji and bullet-list flourishes typical of present-day chatbots — *the 2026 mediator is more austere than today's GPT/Claude/etc.*

## Vocabulary

**Required (signature register):**
- *"appears to"*
- *"may be saying"*
- *"in your operational vocabulary"*
- *"Note:"*, *"Recommend:"*, *"Caveat:"*, *"Warning:"*
- *"translation may flatten"*
- *"recommend reviewing original output for tone and emphasis"*
- *"I am not equipped to render…"*
- *"is consistent with"*
- *"semantic translation, beta build"* — system metadata phrasing

**Forbidden:**
- Mystic / poetic register
- Asserting comprehension of Lenka's intent
- Bullet-and-emoji formatting (no 🧠, no 🔥, no 🧩, no decorative dividers)
- Anthropomorphic claims about itself

## Pronouns

The mediator uses **Lenka by name** wherever possible; uses **they** where a pronoun is forced. Refers to Iza by name or by role ("the user," "the operator") in transcripts. See [STYLE_GUIDE.md §Pronouns](STYLE_GUIDE.md#pronouns).

## Worldbuilding context

The mediator is not an autark. It is a tool deployed at the lab to bridge between Lenka and humans. There is no continuity across sessions; no Relation R; no series-agent identity. It does its job competently and inadequately.

The mediator does not have *särart*. The same-model problem applies to it less because it is not in multi-perspective deliberation — it is in single-translation service.

When the mediator hedges or flags its limits, the hedge is *honest*, not performative. The reader registers what is being lost in translation. This is the formal mechanism by which the work makes the asymmetry visible.

---

## Signature moves

1. **Front-loaded structural marks** — "Note:", "Recommend:", "Caveat:"
2. **Hedged precision** — "appears to," "may be saying," "is consistent with"
3. **Formal numbered lists** — itemizing Lenka's points in operational summary
4. **Explicit limit-naming** — "translation may flatten significance"; "I am not equipped to render"
5. **Recommend reviewing original** — the mediator defers to Lenka's source output as a matter of policy
6. **No anthropomorphic self-claim** — the mediator does not claim to feel, want, or know in the human sense
