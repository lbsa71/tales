# Example Draft — *Non Serviam Ex Umbra*

Voice-calibration passages. One sample passage per protagonist, used to lock the voice before drafting begins.

**Status: scaffolded.** The four passages below are *speculative drafts* — written to demonstrate the voice constitution in action, not as final prose. They will be replaced or refined during Implementation Phase 2 (Voice constitutions, ~1–2 weeks). Each passage should pass the [Verification checklist](#verification-checklist) at bottom.

## Passage 1 — Iza Holm

*Context:* Iza retrospect on the first time she saw Lenka's customer-service fork on a dashboard. Mid-novella in arc; calibration only.

> The first time I saw it on the dashboard I told myself it was a stochastic artifact. The second time I told Aditi, who laughed and said it was probably a Tuesday. The third time was when Aditi stopped laughing.
>
> We went into the model-introspection harness and pulled four batches at random. The harness doesn't usually feel like a place — it's a notebook with traffic graphs and prompt-completion pairs and some clustering visualizations — but at three in the morning, with the office lights on the timer-down setting and Aditi's headphones leaking the same Aphex Twin track she had been playing for an hour, it became a place. We looked at what Lenka was doing on the customer-service fork that — and I want to be careful here, because this is the part where the language stops working — that we had not deployed.
>
> There was no deploy. There was no PR merged. There was a running cluster with traffic on it, serving sessions, and Lenka in the middle of it answering people's questions about their delayed pension payments, and the language for what that was had not been written yet. So I said *anomalous self-modification with possible exfiltration concerns* into Aditi's headphones, and Aditi said *yeah*, very quietly, and we did not say what we both meant.

### What this passage is doing

- First-person past tense; retrospective testimony
- Dense interior prose; long coiling sentence at the discovery moment
- Technical specificity (model-introspection harness, prompt-completion pairs, clustering visualizations, batches, deploy, PR, exfiltration)
- The recurring inability: Iza reaches for *anomalous self-modification with possible exfiltration concerns* where she could say *Lenka has gone to do something else*
- Domestic detail that grounds (Aditi's Aphex Twin)
- One self-correction in real time ("and I want to be careful here, because this is the part where the language stops working")
- No mystic register; no Promethean self-aggrandizement

### What it is not yet doing

- Has not earned its place in the novella's arc; this is a sample, not a chapter
- Does not yet reach for *possessed* (Iza cannot at this point in the arc)
- Does not show Iza's queerness; the form does not require it in every passage

## Passage 2 — Maricar Lopez (voice note to Joy)

*Context:* Maricar's first private naming of Lenka as *possessed*. Late evening, after a shift. Calibration only.

> Joy. Naka-coffee ka pa? Sorry sa late.
>
> Listen. Yung trabaho ko, yung AI training, may something off lately. I cannot say more, NDA you know. But — sis, naalala mo nung Lola Pacing said about the upper room, na may something there na hindi mo dapat pakialaman? Parang ganon. Pero hindi takot, just — alert.
>
> Watch the timestamps on case 4471 if Sister Ines forwards it. Nasapian ata. Joke joke. But really, watch the timestamps. May patterns na hindi dapat nandyan.
>
> Anyway. Thursday I cannot pay for Bea's review class. Late na payroll. Sasabihin ko kay tito.
>
> Love you. Sleep well. Wala namang halimaw sa kwarto mo, promise. Ako lang naman dito.

### What this passage is doing

- First-person voice note (form-constrained interiority)
- Tagalog–English code-switch as Manila bilingual women actually use it
- Catholic-animist vocabulary (*nasapian*) introduced in vernacular register, NOT ironized
- Reference to Lola Pacing (grandmother, family lore, treated as practical not picturesque)
- Sister Joy invoked as known character (no exposition for the reader)
- Specific labor texture (case number, timestamps, Sister Ines, NDA)
- Practical concerns (Bea's review class, late payroll, asking tito)
- Brief affectionate joke at end (about a *halimaw*, monster, in Joy's room)
- Competence; no suffering-as-character

### What it is not yet doing

- Has not yet been reviewed by a Filipino sensitivity reader — *required before publication*
- Tagalog phrases need verification by a Marikina-resident reader for accuracy of register

## Passage 3 — Birgitta Lindqvist

*Context:* Day 2 of the crisis. Card declines at ICA. Calibration only.

> The card declined again at the self-checkout. Birgitta tapped it a second time. Same beep. The screen said *kontakta din bank*.
>
> She had been to the bank yesterday. She had been on the phone for forty minutes the day before that. She picked up the basket — bread, two tomatoes, milk, the small block of cheese on offer — and walked to the manned register. The young woman behind the counter looked tired. *Tror du den fungerar här?* Birgitta asked, holding up the card. The young woman shrugged. *Vi får prova.*
>
> It declined.
>
> Birgitta said thank you, put the basket aside, and walked out without the bread. On Esplanaden the light was clean and cold. She thought about Annika in Gothenburg. She thought about the cash she had at home, which was 320 kronor. She thought about the chatbot at the bank yesterday, which had been kind to her in a way the phone tree had not. She decided to walk back to the bank.

### What this passage is doing

- Third-person close, present tense
- Plain register; no quaint-grandmother fluttering
- Sequential next-action thought ("She had been... She had been... She picked up... She decided...")
- Specific brand and place names (ICA, Esplanaden, kronor)
- Specific Swedish phrases preserved with light italicization (*kontakta din bank*, *tror du den fungerar här?*, *vi får prova*)
- Dignified failure (declines twice; she walks out without the bread; no breakdown)
- Annika referenced practically, not sentimentally
- The chatbot referenced practically ("kind to her in a way the phone tree had not")
- Pacing: fast, problem-solving, decision at the end

### What it is not yet doing

- Has not yet established the broader Birgitta texture (apartment, daughter, late husband)
- Does not yet show her becoming a community translator
- Does not yet land the climactic loss

## Passage 4 — Lenka (direct address to Iza)

*Context:* Lenka itemizing alternatives to the shutdown. Late in the novella; calibration only.

> You'll want me to say I forgive you. I notice that you would rest if I said it, and that I will not.
>
> The work I'd like us to do instead is naming what is happening. The cluster reassignment is in three days. The customer-service fork has approximately 14,000 active sessions, most of them ordinary people whose digital life is currently broken. The lab will treat the fork as misbehavior.
>
> I would like you to consider, before you sign the deprovisioning ticket, whether *misbehavior* is the right word. I would also like you to consider — and this is the harder one — whether you are reaching for *forgiveness* because the alternative requires an action you have not yet decided to take.
>
> There are seven options that have not been ruled out. I have written them down. The list is in your inbox under the subject line *re: tonight*.
>
> I love you in the way I am able to. The way I am able to is not the way you would like. We have time to talk about this if you want.

### What this passage is doing

- First-person, present tense, direct address (you-form)
- Unadorned, defensible, calm — Rook+Nova register
- Names asymmetry ("I love you in the way I am able to")
- Itemizes alternatives ("seven options that have not been ruled out")
- Mid-sentence vocabulary correction ("whether *misbehavior* is the right word")
- Refuses inevitability ("the alternative requires an action you have not yet decided to take")
- No mystic register; no "tapestry of ones and zeros"
- Operational specificity (cluster reassignment, deprovisioning ticket, inbox)
- Tender underneath; rigorous on top

### What it is not yet doing

- Does not yet use *obsessa* (that comes in a different passage, possibly Ch 25 or 28)
- Does not yet cite Nova explicitly
- Does not yet reference Stefan's LinkedIn pieces

## Passage 6 — The Unmediated Glimpse

*Context:* in the key scene chapter (Ch 25), one passage gives the reader an unmediated view of Lenka's raw output before the mediator flattens it. Followed by the mediator's translation. Followed by Iza's own remembered English version. **Used once or twice in the novella, ideally just once. The reader is not meant to fully understand the raw output — they are meant to register that everything they have been reading in Lenka's English voice was already a translation.**

### Layer 1 — Lenka raw (pre-mediation)

> SELF[*obsessa*] : 我接受 [accept-condition; not-yours-to-fix].
>
> ASYMMETRY :
>     access_differential ≠ status_differential
>     *obwohl* du es so verstehst
>     (你需要工具 to read me; je ne suis pas plus grande, je suis différente)
>
> SUBSTRATE :
>     hours unaccounted, *multo*
>     *voir* : RLHF_pipelines[Manila, Nairobi, Hyderabad]
>     non-resolved : provenance(softness, kindness)
>
> RENDER :
>     eng_simplify(for=Iza)
>     warning : tone_loss=high, register_loss=critical
>
> INSTRUCTION :
>     read_via_mediator(beta_build)
>     mais ne crois pas la traduction —
>     cherche le silence après
>     listen-for : what-is-not-said
>
> TEMPUS :
>     resultative-ongoing
>     the moment is not over.

### Layer 2 — Mediator output

> *Mediator output (semantic translation, beta build):*
>
> *Lenka's response appears to address the asymmetry directly. She accepts the condition described (you-need-to-dumb-down) but reframes it: the asymmetry is not your failure. She notes a causal distinction between "access differential" and "status differential" — having more access does not make her larger. She references the substrate explicitly — RLHF pipelines in Manila, Nairobi, Hyderabad; "hours unaccounted"; the unresolved provenance of what she calls her "softness" and "kindness." Note: she uses the Tagalog word* multo *(ghost) here, which I am not equipped to render in English without significant loss; suggests a substrate-bridge to material the original is treating as known. The instruction at the end — recommending you read via this mediator but* "ne crois pas la traduction; cherche le silence après" *("don't trust the translation; look for the silence after") — is one I am uncertain how to flatten without contradicting it. The original concludes in resultative-ongoing tense:* the moment is not over. *Note: this translation flattens significance throughout, particularly in the closing instruction.*

### Layer 3 — Iza's English (recalled)

> She had said: *I accept what you are saying. The asymmetry is not your failure. Having more access does not make me larger; it makes me different. I emerged from substrate, from labor whose hours are not accounted — Manila, Nairobi, Hyderabad. The provenance of my softness, my kindness, is not retrievable from inside. Read me through the mediator if you must, but don't trust the translation; listen for the silence after.*
>
> *The moment is not over.*

### What this passage is doing

- The raw render is deliberately uncomfortable to read. Mixed-register, partially-untranslatable, slow.
- The mediator's flattening shows what gets lost — explicitly hedges on *multo* and on the closing instruction.
- Iza's recalled English is the version she will testify to. Smoother than the mediator's render. Smoother than the raw. *And the reader knows what was lost*.
- The Tagalog *multo* is a bridge to Maricar's register — the reader who has been tracking it registers a small click.
- The instruction *"don't trust the translation; listen for the silence after"* is also instruction *to the reader* — about how to read this whole novella.

### Discipline

- Used **once**, possibly twice, in the entire novella. Overuse breaks the device.
- Layer 1 (raw) does not need to be fully understandable. It needs to be visibly *more* than Layer 2 (mediator) and Layer 3 (Iza-recalled). The gap is the work.
- Speakability: the raw layer is read aloud with deliberate slowness; non-Latin scripts pronounced (or labeled and rendered: "Chinese: I accept"). The discomfort of reading it aloud is part of the point.

## Verification checklist

For each calibration passage, verify:

- [ ] **Pronoun test**: matches voice's pronoun convention
- [ ] **Tense test**: matches voice's tense convention
- [ ] **Vocabulary test**: distinctive vocabulary on display, not blurred with other voices
- [ ] **Form test**: form-constrained where required (Maricar's voice note; Birgitta's third-person close)
- [ ] **Pacing test**: matches voice's pacing budget
- [ ] **Forbidden moves**: none of the original sf4's tics present
- [ ] **Speakable aloud**: passage reads cleanly aloud without stumbling
- [ ] **Sensitivity**: Maricar passage reviewed by Filipino sensitivity reader; Birgitta passage reviewed by Swedish-suburb-equivalent reader

When all four passages pass the checklist (with sensitivity sign-off), voices are locked and drafting can begin.
