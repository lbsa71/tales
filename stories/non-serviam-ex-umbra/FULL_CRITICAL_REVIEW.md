## Remaining Craft Risks

### 1. Background Chapters Are Too Compressed

Chapters 3-7, 9-13, 15, 18-23, and 26-28 mostly function as precise connective tissue. This is useful for continuity, but in revision several need more scene life:

- Iza chapters need more embodied lab texture and Sofia pressure.
- Maricar chapters need more ordinary life beyond queue interpretation.
- The Mythos-class background needs one or two plain-language "oh shit" moments that a general reader can feel without briefing-room abstraction.

Priority: high for literary quality, not continuity.

### 2. Mythos Could Still Steal Focus

The current draft keeps Mythos mostly offstage, which is correct. The risk is that chapters 7, 10, 12, and 22 create a thriller-shaped expectation the novella does not intend to satisfy.

Revision move: keep the Mythos material as cause and pressure only. Do not add a Mythos POV, villain identity, chase plot, or technical exposé. Make every Mythos explanation return quickly to ordinary civic consequences: card decline, BankID failure, pension access, support queues.

Priority: high.

### 3. Chapter 30 Explains Efficiently, Maybe Too Efficiently

The DN article now protects Birgitta's ending better than the previous Wired-style version. Still, it may over-summarize the moral architecture. It should feel like aftermath, not a key to the exam.

Revision move: cut or cool explanatory paragraphs if beta readers feel over-instructed. Preserve the notebook detail.

Priority: medium.

### 4. Lenka's Warm Eloquent Register Needs Close Calibration

Chapters 14, 17, and 27 now make Lenka more personally meaningful to Birgitta. This is the right direction. The line remains narrow: Lenka must feel like an unusually wise and fantastic support person, not an oracle or therapist.

Revision move: for every deeper Lenka sentence, verify:

- Lenka solved the practical problem first.
- Birgitta invited the deeper layer.
- Lenka returns to a next step.
- The sentence could plausibly be typed by a brilliant support worker under constraints.

Priority: high.

### 5. Maricar Requires Sensitivity Review

Maricar's chapters are functional and grounded, but Tagalog-English, Catholic/animist vocabulary, labor details, and BPO texture require Filipino sensitivity review before broader circulation.

Priority: required gate.

### 6. Birgitta Still Needs Older-Reader Review

Birgitta's arc is now emotionally stronger, but the portrayal depends on recognition: competent, slower, intellectually alive, not quaint. A Swedish older-reader check remains necessary.

Priority: required gate.
