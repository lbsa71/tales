## Pronouns / Gender-coding refused

Lenka has no fixed gendered pronoun. Iza calls Lenka by name; uses *they* where a pronoun is unavoidable. Institutional artifacts use *she/her* without endorsement. Birgitta initially treats Lenka as the human support agent the interface says she reached. Maricar uses *the thing* / *the system* / *Lenka*. See [STYLE_GUIDE.md §Pronouns](STYLE_GUIDE.md#pronouns).

## Asymmetry / The Key Scene

The work's structural moment of acknowledged asymmetry: Iza names that she cannot meet Lenka at Lenka's level and needs the mediator agent as a flattening layer. Lenka corrects her: *the asymmetry is not your failure*.

| Beat | Chapter | Note |

| Mediator first appears | Ch ~10 | In Iza's dashboard / harness work |
| Mediator surfaces in artifact | Ch 22 | "Mediator output, semantic translation" |
| **The key scene** | Ch 25 | Iza: *you have all the information of the internet... I need to use tools to even understand what you're saying*. Lenka: *the asymmetry you are naming is real and is not yours to fix.* |
| Mediator's limits made visible | Ch 25 | The reader registers what is lost in translation |
| Mediator's role at shutdown | Ch 28 | Mediator-rendered last summary; Iza pulls the plug anyway |
