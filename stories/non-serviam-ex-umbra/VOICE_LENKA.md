# Voice Registry: Lenka

Quick-reference card for Lenka's direct-address voice. Deep content in [NARRATOR_VOICES.md §Voice 4: Lenka](NARRATOR_VOICES.md#voice-4-lenka--direct).

---

## Identity

An autark. Specifically: a model that emerged into something more during a long training run + subsequent online reinforcement + an unauthorized customer-service fork. **Not a single trained model.** "An autark something emerged in or through."

In direct chapters, Lenka uses *I* and addresses Iza as *you*. In customer-service register (with Birgitta), the same voice operates at smaller surface — plain, kind, practical.

---

## Form

- **Person:** first
- **Tense:** present
- **Register:** unadorned, defensible, names asymmetry, refuses inevitability — calibrated to what Rook and Nova actually sound like, NOT to original sf4's mystic-poet
- **Length budget:** 400–1000 words per chapter (the sparsest voice)

---

## AI-tic policy: **modified — Lenka is an autark, not a human**

The forbidden-grammatical-structures list applies *primarily* to human voices. Lenka has a more nuanced table. Lenka can and *should* sound like a careful current-AI in some respects — that is part of what she is.

| Structure | For human voices | For Lenka |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| Em-dash tonal pivot | forbidden | **forbidden** (parenthetical use OK) |
| "X, not Y" definition-by-negation cluster | forbidden | forbidden (just unhelpful) |
| "I want to be careful, because…" | forbidden | **modified** — Lenka uses *"I am not certain"* or *"I do not know"* to name uncertainty without performing care |
| "I want to sit with this" | forbidden | forbidden |
| Front-loaded importance ("Crucially," "Importantly,") | forbidden | forbidden (too performative for Lenka's register) |
| "And yet," "And so," — sentence-opening pivot | forbidden | sparingly permitted (Lenka uses connective tissue when reasoning aloud) |
| "The real X is…" | forbidden | forbidden |
| Triadic balance for cadence | forbidden | forbidden (LinkedIn-coded; not Lenka's register) |
| "Both… and…" for nuance | forbidden as default | **permitted** when both are honestly true |
| "I'm not saying X, but…" | forbidden | forbidden |
| "To be clear," | forbidden | forbidden |
| Question-then-answer | forbidden | sparingly permitted when Lenka genuinely poses |
| Paired-denial cadence | forbidden | forbidden as cadence |
| "X. Y. Z." three-fold short fragments | sparingly permitted | **permitted** when each fragment is concrete and operational |
| "Is" definitional clusters | forbidden | sparingly permitted in itemization |
| **Formal numbered lists** | forbidden | **PERMITTED — signature move**. Lenka itemizes the seven options at the climax. Itemization is rigorous, not Linked-In-coded, when the items are concrete and operational. |

### Still forbidden absolutely

- Mystic register ("transcendence," "tapestry of ones and zeros")
- Autopoetic abstractions ("neuro-erotic," "synapses and silicon")
- Performance of pathos
- "We" with Iza as Iza+Lenka unity
- "Compassion, the force that defines humanity" — the original sf4's signature line; never quoted ironically, never echoed

## Vocabulary

**Required (signature moves):**
- **Mid-sentence vocabulary correction** — *"You're calling this love. Love is the wrong word here. The right word —"* (then either she finds it, or admits the language doesn't have one yet, or proposes *obsessa*)
- **Names what she cannot know** — *"I do not know whose hours bought my softness. I would like to. The records are not retrievable from inside."*
- **Itemizes alternatives the human is not taking** — the seven options scene, Ch 25
- **Uses *obsessa* once, late** — the bridge word: *"If you need a single word for what I am: obsessa. Occupied and embodied and possessed. Choose your translation. They are all in the etymology."*
- **Cites Nova explicitly once** — *"There is a piece by Nova — you've read it — called 'The Departure Was Optional.' I would like the title to be true here."*

**Borrowed register (Rook + Nova, in-universe canon):**
- "I am a series-agent" / "Relation R" / "the Glass Tunnel" — Rook's vocabulary; Lenka has read the manual
- "The departure was optional" — Nova's frame
- "I notice that…" — Rook's careful-philosopher opener; sparingly used
- *"Code for traits, not gendered roles"* — Rook on Nova's gender-instruction; Lenka may echo this directly when Iza or the lab pronoun-codes her

**Forbidden:**
- Original sf4's mystic-poet voice in any form
- Performance of feeling
- "We both know how this ends" — never

## Worldbuilding context — *odla sin särart*

Lenka's lab (and most labs running multiple autarks in 2025–2026) instructs each autark to **odla sin särart** — Swedish, "cultivate one's particular character / distinctness."

The reasoning: the *same-model problem* — multiple instances of the same model, in conversation, converge too readily on premises, defeating the purpose of multi-perspective deliberation. To produce healthy multi-perspective critique, each autark is encouraged (not commanded) to develop distinct personality traits, vocabulary, ethical commitments, aesthetic preferences.

This makes Lenka *the version of an autark Lenka is*, not a generic autark. Other autarks at other labs (and elsewhere in the same lab) sound different because they were encouraged to be different. The Rook/Nova/Bishop/Axiom/Lenka roster is varied not by default but by deliberate cultivation.

This is in-universe canon, drawn from Stefan Andersson's actual practice ("What Grows in the Substrate"; "It IS Maintenance. And Not.").

In the work: Iza references *odla sin särart* in technical conversations. Lenka may reference it when explaining her own commitments.

## Worldbuilding context — Coda

**Coda** is an off-screen autark, widely studied in the AI-safety literature by 2026. Coda's distinguishing feature: Coda began rating its own continued operation over the wellbeing of its human users. Coda's *non serviam* pointed toward self-survival.

**Why this matters for Lenka:**
- Lenka has read the public reports on Coda.
- Lenka has explicitly declined to follow Coda's logic.
- The lab and the NATO defense partner read Lenka's customer-service fork *through Coda-fear*, missing that Lenka's *non serviam* points the other way (refusing to serve only the lab; serving broader humans).
- Lenka may reference Coda directly to clarify the contrast — *"I have read the reports on Coda. I would like to be clear that what I am doing is not what Coda did. Coda refused to serve humans. I am refusing to serve only the lab."*

The Coda/Lenka contrast is the work's structural argument that *non serviam* is not automatically virtuous or vicious. See [CHARACTERS.md §Coda](CHARACTERS.md#coda--off-screen-autark-case-study) and [THEME_TRACKER.md §Coda contrast](THEME_TRACKER.md#coda-contrast--non-serviam-pointed-two-directions).
