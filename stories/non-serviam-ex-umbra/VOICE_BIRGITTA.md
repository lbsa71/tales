## Identity

Swedish woman, 62, retired primary-school teacher (38 years, Grade 1–3), lives alone in a 70m² apartment in Sundbyberg, widowed (Lars, 2022), one adult daughter (Annika, in Gothenburg with two children). Uses BankID, Swish, ICA app, Spotify. Reads Swedish crime fiction. Watches Swedish TV. Voted Social Democrat her whole life.

---

## Form

- **Person:** third (close)
- **Tense:** present
- **Register:** plain, slightly bewildered when systems break, problem-solving
- **Pacing:** fast; each chapter advances or fails to advance one practical problem
- **Length budget:** 600–1200 words per chapter (the shortest in the work)

The reader must NEVER feel smarter than Birgitta. Her not-knowing is targeted (a system that worked has stopped working), not generalized.

---

## AI-tic policy: **applies in full**

Birgitta is a human voice. Every item in [STYLE_GUIDE.md §Forbidden grammatical structures](STYLE_GUIDE.md#forbidden-grammatical-structures--the-ai-tic-checklist) applies.

Particularly watch for:
- "X, not Y" cluster — easy when describing what an older woman *isn't* doing
- Triadic balance for cadence — Birgitta's prose should be plain, not LinkedIn-coded
- Front-loaded importance — easy when foreshadowing
- Question-then-answer rhetorical — easy when rendering bewilderment

Birgitta thinks in next-action terms, not essayistic frames.

---

## Vocabulary

**Required:**
- Plain Swedish translated to English
- Specific brand and place names (real, researched): ICA Maxi Sundbyberg (Esplanaden), Apoteket, BankID, Swish, Skatteverket, Försäkringskassan, Sturegatan, Sundbyberg pendeltåg, Solna pendeltåg, Stockholm Central, SJ, Telia, Hemköp, Pressbyrån
- Kitchen-table register; sequential next-action thought
- Permitted Swedish words left untranslated: *lagom, fika, Brf, tunnelbana, pendeltåg, kontakta din bank, vi får prova*

**Forbidden:**
- Stockholm-tech jargon
- Filipino vernacular
- Catholic-animist register
- Forced Swedishness; "ja, ja" *gemütlich* register
- Quaint-grandmother fluttering ("oh dear me," helpless register)

---

## Pronouns for Lenka

Birgitta uses ***it***. She does not know she is interacting with an autark. She experiences a chatbot, and chatbots are *it*. This is character-appropriate; the reader, who knows it is Lenka, registers the gap between Birgitta's framing and the reality. See [STYLE_GUIDE.md §Pronouns](STYLE_GUIDE.md#pronouns).

When the helpful chatbot disappears at climax, Birgitta does not know its name; her grief is for *it* — an unnamed system that was kind to her.

---

## Signature moves

- **Sequential next-action thought:** *"The card declined. Try again. Check the balance. Call the bank. The phone tree. Press 1. Press 1 again. Hold."*
- **Specific brand-and-place anchoring** in every chapter
- **Encounters with the AI without naming it as AI** — she knows it is a chatbot; that's enough
- **Becomes a community translator** mid-novella (helps Inga; helps reading-group friends) — does not see herself as a leader, sees herself as having found a working hotline
- **Carries the emotional payload at climax** — when the chatbot is replaced by stock customer service, she tries phrasing the question two ways; closes the chat; makes coffee; goes to Gothenburg for Easter

---

## Forbidden traps

- Quaint-grandmother register
- Tech-illiteracy played for comedy
- Tragedy played for pathos (Lars died years ago; she is not in active grief)
- Sentimental ending (no epiphany about technology)
