Reader expectation for this draft:

- Chapter 1 should raise suspicion about an unnamed lab system and a banking-support anomaly without revealing Lenka.
- Chapters 2, 8, and 11 should make Birgitta's competence and exhaustion legible before any humane support appears.
- Chapters 14 and 17 should let the reader form the relation with Lenka through practical relief, not ontology.
- Chapters 24 and 25 should reclassify Lenka's help as unauthorized without making Lenka feel like the cause of the original banking crisis.
- Chapter 29 should carry the wound: Birgitta asks for Lenka and the institution has no category for the person who helped her.
- Chapter 30 should explain enough after the fact without taking the ending away from Birgitta.
