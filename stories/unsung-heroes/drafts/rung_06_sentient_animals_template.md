# Chapter 6: Sentient Animals

**Target Word Count**: 500-800 words
**Time Period**: ~500 million years ago (complex brains), varying by species
**Mode of Competition**: Behavioral strategy — what chooses well, wins

---

## Writing Notes

### Core Description (~200-400 words)
- What they were: Animals with nervous systems, sensory organs, behavioral choices
- How they operated: Learning, strategy, real-time decision making
- What they couldn't conceive: Abstraction, symbolic thinking, representation

### Transition Observation (~100-200 words)
- How they were superseded: By humans with abstract thinking
- Introduction of new dimension: Modeling reality, representation
- Pattern reinforcement: Choice introduces mistake; abstraction introduces misjudgment

### Narrator's Reflection (~100-200 words)
- What this means: First true "minds" — entities that can be wrong
- Connection to larger pattern: Choice creates new failure modes
- Emotional/philosophical weight: Recognition of the first minds

---

## Key Observations to Weave In
- "Creatures with spines and eyes and tongues and fear"
- "Strategy became the new currency of survival"
- "Creatures who chose poorly vanished"
- "Those who learned—truly learned—carved out time in the fossil record"
- "A ceaseless refinement of instinct"

## Voice Reminders

- More emotional engagement
