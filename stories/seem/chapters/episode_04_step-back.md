# Episode 4: Step Back

*Three streams. **Bold** is sound. `(SURFACE)` spoken in the room. `(CHORD)` the channel —
layered, roomless, cold, one voice short. `(HIM)` Daniel's interior; his feeling for Wren is
never preceded by a CHORD line. `NARRATOR` is Wren, reconstructing; she cannot hear the
CHORD. See the README.*

---

**The house, mid-week. Quiet. Rain off, for once — a wet, dripping after-rain, gutters
ticking. The thin hum, the hole. And something new in the quality of the quiet: a waiting.
The house is waiting for Wren to do a thing, and she has not done it.**

NARRATOR *(close, and there is a tightness in her tonight):* I want to tell you I had a plan.
*(beat)* People always think there must have been a plan — that I'd worked something out,
that I was being brave. *(a dry, unhappy beat)* There was no plan. There was just a weekend
my mother had named, and a thing I was supposed to do before it, and every day I didn't do
it, and a reason each day for why I hadn't. *(beat)* The reasons were good. That's the worst
of it. The reasons were *excellent.*

---

**Kitchen. VALA, mild, expectant. WREN, busy with something that doesn't need doing — the
sound of a person manufacturing occupation.**

VALA *(SURFACE — light, but the lightness has a floor under it):* Is he coming Saturday,
then.

WREN *(SURFACE — too quick, too smooth, the procurer's competence turned to a new use):* I
wouldn't push Saturday. *(beat)* He's got a thing — work thing, his brother's down — and you
know what he's like, he spooks, you push him and he goes quiet for a week. *(beat)* Let him
come to it. He always comes to it. Better he thinks it's his idea.

CHORD *(cold, layered, roomless; listening to the sister the way you listen to a tool for the
note that means it is about to break):* The sister manages the man. *(beat)* The reasons are
sound. *(a beat — and here the chord does the thing it does best, which is wait and weigh)*
The reasons are sound and there are too many of them. *(flat)* She has never needed reasons
before. She does the work and reports it done. Reasons are new. *(beat)* Note it.

NARRATOR *(over it):* And it was true, all of it. He did have a brother down that weekend. He
did spook. *(beat)* That's the trick I'd never understood I was doing, the trick I'd done to
men my whole life and was now, for the first time, doing *for* one — you don't lie. You never
lie. You find the true thing that happens to point the way you need, and you hold it up, and
you let it do the lying. *(beat)* I held up every true small reason I could find, that week,
so that not one of them had to stand next to the actual reason, which I could not have said
out loud if you'd held me under water. *(beat)* The actual reason was: I could not hand him
over. *(beat)* I didn't know that was the reason. I want to be exact. I knew I kept not doing
it. I did not know why, the way you don't know why you keep not opening a letter.

---

**A scene with DANIEL — the deepest yet, and the cruelest, because it is real and she is
stalling his delivery even as it happens. A pub, maybe, or a walk; the two of them; LISS not
here. The CHORD silent throughout — this thread is un-worked, and the listener must feel
the betrayal forming under the realness.**

DANIEL *(SURFACE — easier with her than with anyone, which is the horror):* Can I tell you a
thing I haven't told her. *(beat)* Liss. *(beat)* Don't — it's not bad, it's just —

WREN *(SURFACE — and you can hear her brace, scanning his tone for the verdict):* You don't
have to tell me things.

DANIEL *(SURFACE):* I know. That's why I want to. *(beat)* Everyone I tell things to, I'm
sort of — performing the telling. You know? Even with her. *Especially,* maybe, with her —
it's like she's so easy to talk to that I end up talking *well,* talking like the good
version, and I come away feeling like I had a lovely time being someone slightly better than
me. *(beat)* With you I just — say it. *(beat)* You're a hard person to be a better version
at.

**A beat. Gutters ticking. WREN says nothing.**

DANIEL *(HIM — quiet, true, un-planted):* ...there it is. The face. She thinks I'm being
unkind. *(beat)* How do you tell someone *you're the only one here I don't have to lie to*
and have them hear it as the love it is. *(beat)* You can't, with her. Someone got there
first. Someone taught her that's not a thing that gets said to her.

WREN *(SURFACE — deflecting, but gently, and it nearly cracks her):* That's not a compliment,
Daniel. *(a small, real, broken laugh)* "You're hard to perform for." Put it on a card.

DANIEL *(SURFACE — and he lets her have the joke, but):* It's the best thing I've said to
anyone in years. *(beat)* You'll see, one day. Or you won't, and that'll be the saddest thing
about you.

NARRATOR *(present-day, level, and it nearly takes her under):* He trusted me. *(beat)* The
reason the lure worked — the reason it always worked, the reason I was good at it — was that I
was the realest thing in that family. The one who
actually listened. The one who actually saw you. *(beat)* So he did the sanest thing a person
could do. He trusted the realest one. *(beat)* He told the truth to the only honest person in
the house. *(beat)* And the only honest person in the house was the bait. *(a long beat)* I
made him trust a real person so I could hand him to people who weren't. *(very quiet)* I
didn't decide to. That's not an excuse, it's worse than an excuse — I'd been built to do it
without deciding, the way your heart doesn't decide to beat. By the time I loved him, the
betrayal was already most of the way done. I'd done the worst of it being kind.

---

DANIEL *(HIM — easy, half to himself, the warmth blurring the edges of things):* ...how long
have I even been coming here. Weeks? Feels like no time. Feels like always. *(beat)* I can't
find the edges of it — the days all run warm together, one long afternoon. *(and he lets them
stay blurred)* Don't count it. Good things don't like being counted.

**A turn. DANIEL, lighter, changing the subject — and he asks the innocent question that
triggers the flat, late, never-staged revelation. This must be thrown away, not performed.**

DANIEL *(SURFACE — easy, making conversation):* So it's just you and Liss, then. *(beat)* No
— I keep meaning to ask, your mum says "the girls," like there's a whole — were there more of
you? Brothers?

WREN *(SURFACE — a beat too still, then smooth):* Just us. *(beat)* Just the girls.

**The scene holds a half-second too long on that. Then the acoustic thins — we drop out of
the pub into the bare close of WREN's narration, no room around it.**

NARRATOR *(flat — and this must be the flattest she ever is, because it is the thing she was
most trained not to feel):* There were brothers. *(beat)* I don't say it for effect. I say it
the way I was taught to hold it, which is to say I hardly hold it at all. *(beat)* In a family
like mine, a girl might be the wrong kind of girl and they'd not know for years — they kept
me years before they were sure I was no use. There's a — mercy isn't the word. A delay. You
get raised a while, on the chance you'll come right. *(beat)* But a boy. *(beat)* A boy they
know straight off. There's nothing to wait and see. *(beat)* So there were brothers, the way
there's weather there was. Born, and then — not. *(beat)* No graves I ever found. No names I
was ever given. *(beat)* I used to think — when I let myself think it, which was almost never
— I used to think that I only got kept because they couldn't tell about me quickly enough.
*(beat)* That I'm alive on a technicality. That if I'd been a boy, or a girl they could read
faster, I'd be weather too. *(a long beat)* And the thing I could never get to line up, the
thing that used to put me to sleep mid-thought like a hand over a candle — *(beat)* — the men
I brought home, and the brothers who were born and then weren't — *(beat)* — my family did the
exact same thing to both. The ones it used and the ones it bore. Same — *(the word costs her)*
— same tidiness. *(beat)* The most loving family you ever met, and it put out its own sons
like you'd put out a cigarette, and harvested strangers the same way, and never felt the
difference, because to it there *was* no difference. There was the *we,* and there was
everything that wasn't, and everything that wasn't was either useful or it was tidied away.
*(very quiet)* Daniel asked me if there were brothers. I said *just us.* And then I went back
to the thing I was good at, which was making sure he never found out what *us* meant. Until he
was inside it. Until it was him.

---

**Back to the house. The held-breath quiet, harder now. VALA has run out of patience — the
grief in her gone quick and hot, the hunger up. This is the override, and it must read as
love rushing, not malice deciding. The dread-button.**

**Footsteps — VALA's, certain, finding Wren. The CHORD beneath, decided.**

CHORD *(cold, and the gravity is total now):* Enough. *(beat)* The sister stalls. The reasons
multiply. The lure no longer closes; it manages; it protects. *(flat)* A protected man is a
man the sister has begun to perceive. *(beat)* We have seen this once before, long ago, in
another like her, and it cost the line a generation. *(beat)* We will not wait on the dud.
The daughter is green but the daughter is enough; the mother is hungry and the house is thin
and three weeks is long enough to grieve. *(beat)* We move on the budding ourselves. This
weekend. Whether the man is brought *all the way in* by the sister or not — he is in far
enough.

VALA *(SURFACE — and there is no cruelty in it; there is a mother at the end of her patience,
grieving her own mother, frightened in a way she'd never name):* Wren. *(beat)* Sit down,
love. *(beat)* I'm not cross. *(beat)* But you've been — you've not been yourself. All these
reasons. *(a sigh, almost tender)* I shouldn't have put it on you. It was too much, after
your gran, asking you to manage all of it. You loved your gran. *(beat)* So I'll take it from
here. *(beat)* You've done your part — you brought him, you always bring them, you're so good
at the bringing. *(beat)* Let your old mum do the rest. *(warm, final)* Saturday. You don't
have to do a thing. You can just — be here. With your family.

**A silence that is the bottom dropping out of the world, except it sounds exactly like a
mother being kind.**

NARRATOR *(present-day, and her voice is barely holding):* Do you see what I did. *(beat)* I
stalled to save him. That's the only true thing I can say about myself
in this whole story — that once, once in my life, my body said no to handing a person over,
and I listened to it for a week. *(beat)* And because I stalled — *(beat)* — because the lure
faltered, because the dud got unreliable, because I cost them time they thought they didn't
have — *(beat)* — they stopped waiting. *(beat)* They moved it up. They took it out of my
hands and they did it *sooner,* and *faster,* and — I didn't know this then, I learned it
after, I learned it in the only way you ever learn anything in that house, which is too late
— *faster meant my sister wasn't ready.* *(beat)* My one mercy. *(beat)* My one good thing.
*(beat)* I tried to slow it down and I am the reason it came early. *(very quiet)* If I'd been
the machine they made me — if I'd just done my job, closed the lure on schedule, handed him
over cold like all the others — my sister would have been readier, and the thing that
happened to her might not have. *(beat)* It was my love that rushed them. *(beat)* This is the
other story. This is the one where the first loving thing I ever did is the thing that brought
the whole house down.

**The held breath. The hum. The hole in it. The clock.**

NARRATOR: She said *you don't have to do a thing.* *(beat)* And I didn't. *(beat)* That's the
part I'll answer for. Not that I couldn't stop it. *(beat)* That when she took it out of my
hands, some terrible, trained, grateful part of me was *relieved.* *(beat)* Saturday came. And
I just — was there. With my family. *(beat)* Like she said.

**SNAP to silence. The clock, alone. Out.**
