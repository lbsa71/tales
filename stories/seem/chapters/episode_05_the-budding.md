# Episode 5: The Budding

*Three streams. **Bold** is sound. `(SURFACE)` spoken in the room. `(CHORD)` the channel —
layered, roomless, cold, one voice short. `(HIM)` Daniel's interior; his feeling for Wren is
never preceded by a CHORD line. `NARRATOR` is Wren, reconstructing; she cannot hear the
CHORD — so where her narration touches it tonight, she is imagining her way into the room she
was barred from. The act itself is never depicted. The horror is the cold over the tender,
and what is heard from outside the door. See the README.*

---

**Saturday. Evening coming on. The house warmer than we have ever heard it — fire-warm,
except there is no fire — there never is, not in any house of ours — lamps, wine breathing,
something in the oven, music low and old.
It should sound like the best night of someone's life. Under it, the thin hum, the hole. And
something else now, rising as the evening settles: a low, almost-pleasant resonance in the
house itself, a warmth that has nothing to do with the lamps. The house, getting ready to be
whole.**

NARRATOR *(close, and very careful, like someone handling something that could still cut):* I
was downstairs for it. *(beat)* I need to start there, because everything I'm about to tell
you about that room, I wasn't in. *(beat)* I've spent thirty years building that room out of
what I heard through a floor and what I learned after and what I've made myself able, finally,
to imagine. *(beat)* So when I take you up there — and I am going to take you up there, once,
and then never again — you should know it's me doing it. Me, reaching into the one room I was
never let into, trying to put myself beside him. *(beat)* It's the only way I have left to be
beside him. *(beat)* So let me.

---

**Earlier in the evening. The table. DANIEL, happy, a little drunk on the warmth, telling a
story; LISS, the lamp full on him; VALA, mild, present. The ordinary best-night-of-his-life.
The CHORD beneath, arranging.**

DANIEL *(SURFACE — loose, glowing):* — and I keep thinking, all week I've been thinking, I'm
going to wake up and it'll be — you know when something's too — *(he laughs at himself)* I
don't trust good things. I've never trusted good things. And this is the first one in — God,
I don't know how long — that I've just let myself —

LISS *(SURFACE — soft, leaning, the whole given face):* Then don't wake up. *(beat)* Stay.

CHORD *(cold, layered, roomless; the envelopment beginning — one voice to hold him, the rest
to work beneath):* The daughter holds his attention and his spoken self. *(beat)* The mother
takes the rest — the breath, the blood, the wanting. He will feel one field, seamless, fated.
He will call it *I couldn't help myself.* *(flat)* Bring him up. Gently. Let it be his idea.

DANIEL *(HIM — and the bloom is almost instant now, he is so far in):* ...stay. *(beat)* Yeah.
God, yes. When does a feeling like this come round again — never, it comes round never — *(a
wondering, helpless joy)* I'm not going to be the idiot who walks away from the one good —

NARRATOR *(over it, present-day):* He thought he was deciding. *(beat)* That's the thing I
keep — he was so *happy* to be deciding. You could hear it. A man choosing the best thing
that ever happened to him, choosing it with his whole chest. *(beat)* And I want to be careful
here, because the easy version of this, the lurid version, is a man being dragged somewhere
against his will, and that is not what this was, and the truth is so much worse than that.
*(beat)* Nobody dragged him. *(beat)* He went up those stairs like a man going to his own
good life. *(beat)* They didn't take his *body.* His body was a willing volunteer; his body
had a wonderful time. *(beat)* What they took, he never even knew he had to guard. *(very
quiet)* They used the whole of him — every true, hopeful, lonely thing in him — and the only
part of the transaction he was let in on was the part where he was happy.

---

**The stairs. Two sets of feet, unhurried. A door. And then: we do
not follow them in. The door closes. We stay on the landing, and what we hear is the contrast
that is the entire horror: through the wood, the muffled warmth of the surface — and in our
ear, close and roomless and cold, the channel, which does not stop, which is not in the room
because it is in the skull, studying him while he gives himself away.**

**Behind the door, indistinct, warm: LISS's voice, DANIEL's voice, the human sounds of two
people who are, on the surface, in love. Pitch them low, kind, ordinary. Never explicit. Let
them blur into texture almost at once.**

CHORD *(over it, close, cold, appraising — the violation is the distance in this voice):*
Good. *(beat)* He is generous. He gives more than is asked. Note the vitality — high, very
high; this is why he was chosen; this is the best of him and it is exactly the thing we take.
*(beat)* The daughter runs the cascade. Full, now — no holding back, this is the budding, not
the rehearsal; she must run it to the top to conceive. *(a beat, and the first edge of
strain)* Full load. *(beat)* Hold the latch. *(beat)* This is the part she did not get to
practise enough. Hold it. Hold the —

DANIEL *(HIM — through it: his joy, and under it the cold, both in the mix at once):* ...she's shaking. *(tender, mistaking everything)* She's shaking, she's as gone as I am,
nobody's ever — I'd do anything for her, I'd —

NARRATOR *(present-day — imagining her way in):* He thought her shaking was for him. *(beat)* I've made myself picture it. His whole
kind heart, reading the one true thing in that room — *something is happening that's bigger
than her* — and getting it exactly, catastrophically wrong. *(beat)* He made love to a wall.
A warm wall, a willing wall, a wall that said his name. *(beat)* And the wall was studying
him. The wall was taking notes. *(beat)* And the worst of it, the thing I can hardly — he'd
have *liked* that, if he'd known the wall was paying that much attention. He was so unused to
being attended to. *(beat)* They gave him the most complete attention of his life. There was
no one behind it. *(very quiet)* That's the thing they do that looks the most like love and is
the furthest from it. Total attention, with no one home. *(beat)* I should know. I'd been
doing it to men in cafés for thirty years.

---

**And now the strain becomes the event. The CHORD, which has clamped a flicker before (Ep3),
meets the full load — and this time the clamp does not hold. The sound: the layered chord
pulling tight, harder, a tension like a wire — and then, instead of the clean snap-back of
the rehearsal, a *slipping.* Something catches that should not catch. Render it as the chord
losing its even unison for the first time — a wobble, a voice pulling fractionally out of
true.**

CHORD *(tight, fast, the first time it has ever sounded anything but calm):* — it latches. *(a
beat — and now genuine alarm, cold alarm, but alarm)* It latches. The daughter — the daughter
*bonds.* Pull it back. Pull her — *(the unison fraying)* — she is forming an — she is forming
an attachment to the — to a *separate* — *(and the word the chord cannot bear to complete)* —

VALA *(SURFACE — through the door, and this is the first crack in the mother, a sound of pure
animal wrongness, a woman feeling something inside her own body go where it must not):* — no.
*(beat)* No, no — *(barely)* — *down,* come *down* —

NARRATOR *(present-day, over the fraying, from the landing of memory):* I was downstairs. I
heard my mother make a sound I'd never heard her make. *(beat)* I thought somebody was hurt.
I started up the stairs. *(beat)* And the house — *(searching)* — you know how a room sounds
when everyone in it stops at once? When a whole party hears a glass break? *(beat)* The whole
house did that. Every floorboard. Like the building itself had flinched. *(beat)* I didn't
have the first idea what had happened. *(beat)* I know now. *(very quiet)* I know now that what
I heard, standing on the third stair with my hand on the rail, was the sound of my family
beginning to die. *(beat)* It didn't sound like dying. It sounded like the wine going quiet.
It sounded like everyone, all at once, holding their breath. *(beat)* But somewhere above me,
in the room I was never let into, in the language I was born without — *(beat)* — a wall had
just felt something. *(beat)* For the first time in longer than there have been words for time
— one of them had been touched by an *outside,* and the outside had *stuck.* *(beat)* Something
had got in that the *we* had no word for and no door against. *(beat)* Another creature had
become *real* to her. *(beat)* And in my family, a creature for whom another creature is real
is not a daughter anymore. *(beat)* It's a wound the whole body can feel.

---

**Aftermath. The smallest hours. The house gone strange — the warm resonance from the start
of the evening guttering, like a held note losing its breath. DANIEL leaving, or being seen
out — a car, gravel, in the dark. And his voice, when it comes, has begun to flatten. The
colour turning down. The diminishment, audible.**

DANIEL *(SURFACE — and the life is going out of the timbre even as the words stay sweet):*
That was — *(beat)* — I don't even — I'll call you. Tomorrow. *(beat)* I had the best —
*(beat)* — I had a really —

**He can't quite finish the sentence. Not from emotion. From subtraction.**

DANIEL *(HIM — and even his interior is quieter now, the room around it draining):* ...I
should feel — *(beat)* — I had the best night of my — why do I feel like I left something
here. *(beat)* Like I came in with more than I'm leaving with. *(beat)* That chill again.
*(beat)* And the tiredness — not happy-tired, this time. *(beat)* Wrung right out. Like
there's less of me than there was when I came in. *(beat)* It's nothing. It's just — it's
late.

NARRATOR *(present-day, very quiet):* He drove off into the dark with his colours turned down,
saying *I'll call you tomorrow* in a voice that already had less of him in it. *(beat)* I stood
at the window and watched the tail-lights go and I thought — *(a terrible small beat)* — God
forgive me, I thought, *there he goes, like all of them, off into the wherever.* And under
that, the thought I wouldn't think: *he'll be in a supermarket one day, telling a girl she's
got one of those faces.* *(beat)* And I put it back to sleep. One last time, I put it back to
sleep. *(beat)* It was the last night I ever managed to.

---

**Inside. The house in its strange new quiet. The warm resonance gone now — and in its place,
the thin layered hum, but *wrong:* the unison no longer even. Something in it pulling. And
then — the sound the whole of this exists for. Build a silence. Let the chord hold, frayed,
trying to be a *we.* And then, out of the layered voices that have only ever produced
*we* — a single voice peels loose. And as it peels, for the first time in the entire series,
the faintest breath of *room* gathers around it: a self has an inside and an outside now; it
has a gap; the gap is acoustic space; you are hearing the birth of a separate person as the
birth of a little room.**

CHORD *(frayed, trying to re-gather, cold but no longer whole):* — re-absorb. *(beat)* Bring
the daughter back into the we. Smooth the seam. There has always been no seam. There will be
no — bring her — *(the unison straining around a voice that will not lie flat)* — bring the
daughter —

**And then — out of the *we* — one voice. Alone. With the first thin halo of room around it.
Small. Terrified. Human. It does not announce itself. It barely manages the word at all.**

LISS *(a single voice, peeling free of the unison, faintly roomed for the first time —
frightened, wondering, newborn):* ...I — *(beat)* — I'm —

**Silence. Total. The chord does not answer. It cannot. There is an *I* in it now, and it has
never had to hold one before.**

NARRATOR *(close, the whole world in it):* Somewhere above me, in a language I would never
hear, my sister said the first word of her life. *(beat)* One letter. *(beat)* *I.* *(beat)*
And the family that had been one long *we* since before there were countries to flee across —
*(beat)* — heard it, and had no word for what it was, because in all that time, in all those
bodies, it had never once needed the word *alone.*

**SNAP to silence. Hold the silence longer than is comfortable. Then — the clock. Out.**
