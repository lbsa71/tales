# Episode 3: The Hard Week

*Three streams. **Bold** is sound. `(SURFACE)` spoken in the room. `(CHORD)` the channel —
layered, roomless, cold, one voice short. `(HIM)` Daniel's interior; his feeling for Wren is
never preceded by a CHORD line. `NARRATOR` is Wren, reconstructing; she cannot hear the
CHORD. See the README.*

---

**The house, a week on. It has filled out a little — there is a man in it now, on and off,
and you can hear how a house changes when someone is being made welcome: more chairs moved,
a second pot of coffee, music low. But under the welcome, the thin layered hum, and the hole
in it, unchanged. The amputation does not heal. It only gets dressed for company.**

NARRATOR *(close):* He started coming most days. *(beat)* That's fast, I know. I thought so
too, at the time — I had a whole speech ready about how he shouldn't, how it was too quick,
how he barely knew us. I never gave it. *(a small dry beat)* You don't slow a thing down when
the slowing-down would mean admitting you can see where it's going.

**Daniel and Liss, somewhere in the house, laughing — that easy, established rhythm now.**

NARRATOR: And he was *happy.* That's the part I can't get round, even now. I'd watch him
come up the path with his shoulders down out of his ears for once, this man who folded his
receipts to nothing, and he'd be — light. Whatever we were doing to him, it didn't feel to
him like being done to. It felt like being chosen. *(beat)* I knew that feeling. I lived for
that feeling. *(beat)* I should have known sooner what it cost. I, of all people, should have
known what it costs to feel chosen by us.

---

**A room with the two of them — Liss and Daniel, close, intimate, the courtship at its
warmest. Wine. Rain returning at the window. And underneath, for the first time, we hear the
suppression as a live thing: the CHORD working Liss like a hand on a dial, and a flicker
where it almost slips.**

DANIEL *(HIM — settling into the warmth, and under it a thread he can't place):* ...God, I'm
tired. *(beat)* I come up that path light as anything and drive home wrung right out. Sleep
ten hours and wake like I've not slept at all. *(he smiles it off)* Happy-tired. That's a
thing, though, isn't it. I'd forgotten what it is to have something worth being worn out for.

LISS *(SURFACE — low, warm, the lamp turned full on him):* Stay tonight. *(beat)* I'm not
being — I just don't want the day to be over. Is that mad.

DANIEL *(SURFACE — undone, gentle):* It's not mad. *(beat)* God. No.

CHORD *(cold, layered, roomless; precise, supervisory):* Run it warm. *(beat)* Bring the
breath up. Let the body answer. *(a fraction sharper)* The body answers — hold the latch
down. Hold it. The warmth is the tool. The warmth is not *yours.* You do not keep what you
spend.

**A pause. And then — small, almost nothing, a half-second where LISS's breath catches in a
way that isn't performance. The CHORD reacts — the nearest it comes to alarm: not louder,
*faster,* clamping.**

CHORD *(quick, hard):* — there. *(flat, immediate)* There. It reaches. Down. *(a beat, then
cold control restored)* Down. *(slower now)* The daughter is under-rehearsed. The eldest
would have caught that sooner. *(the gap where Edda is not — a half-beat of nothing)* The
eldest is not here to catch it. We are short, and the daughter is green, and we proceed
anyway, because the mother will not wait. Note the risk. Continue.

NARRATOR *(present-day, over it, and she is guessing into the room she was shut out of):* I
wasn't in the room for that. *(beat)* I'm imagining it — I want to be honest that I'm
imagining it, because the temptation, when you've had thirty years, is to pretend you saw
everything. I didn't see this. *(beat)* But I heard, through the wall, my sister's breath do
a thing. *(beat)* One sound. Like a swimmer who thinks, for half a length, that they might
not make the far side. And then nothing — she made it, she always made it — and the wine
went on, and the low laughing. *(beat)* I lay in the dark next door and I thought: *she's so
easy with people. Everything's so easy for her.* *(very quiet)* I'd have screamed if I'd
known what that one caught breath was. *(beat)* It was the first finger-hold of the thing
that was going to take her. And I lay there and envied it.

---

**Morning. Kitchen. The ordinary clatter. And now a true small scene between WREN and
DANIEL — nothing is planted here. The CHORD is silent throughout: the seam-free thread, the
one real thing.**

DANIEL *(SURFACE — easy, drying a cup he wasn't asked to dry):* She's still asleep. Your
mum's — I don't actually know where your mum goes. She's like weather. She's just suddenly
in a room.

WREN *(SURFACE — careful, a little startled to be spoken to plainly):* You get used to it.

DANIEL *(SURFACE):* Do you, though. *(beat — gentle, not prying)* You don't seem used to
anything in this house. You seem like a guest who's been here longest.

**A beat. Water running. WREN doesn't answer that.**

DANIEL *(HIM — quiet, true, un-planted; the real one):*
...don't push it. She'll bolt. *(beat)* She's lovely, the sister. She's the loveliest one
and she doesn't know it and somebody's seen to it that she'll never find out. *(beat)* It
makes me — I don't know what it makes me. Sad. Sad in a way that wants to do something about
it.

WREN *(SURFACE — deflecting, drying her hands):* You're good at this. The dishes. *(beat)*
Most men who come here don't —

DANIEL *(SURFACE — light, but he means it):* Most men who come here are here for your sister.
*(beat)* I came back for your sister. *(beat)* But I keep ending up in the kitchen with you.

**Silence. The tap, off. Rain.**

NARRATOR *(present-day, and it costs her everything to say it level):* There it is again.
*(beat)* And I heard *I keep ending up in the kitchen with you* — and turned it, before it
could land, into *he feels sorry for me.* *(beat)* It wasn't modesty. *(beat)* I don't know
how to — it was faster than modesty. Any warmth pointed my way, something in me caught it and
set it down again as a mistake, or a thing somebody wanted. Every time. *(beat)* So a man
stood in my kitchen and told me the truest thing anyone had said to me in years, and I filed
it under *mistake,* and dried my hands, and went to wake my sister so he could go and be with
the person he'd come for. *(very quiet)* I've wondered, since, what he heard in that. Whether
he learned, that morning, that there was no point. *(beat)* I taught men there was no point in
me. I was so good at it I taught the only one who meant it.

---

**Later. WREN low — you can hear it in her, the supermarket man still on her, the grief, the
tiredness. VALA finds her. And this is the feeding: rendered as the tenderest thing in the
episode, an embrace — and Wren's voice comes out of it *greyer,* a notch of colour gone.**

VALA *(SURFACE — warm, real, a mother):* Oh, look at you. *(beat)* Come here. Come here to
me. You've gone all — you get like this, you always have, since you were tiny. Come here.

**An embrace. Real, in the room — the rustle of it, a hand on hair. It should sound like
exactly what Wren has wanted her whole life. And under it, the CHORD, cold, doing something
else entirely.**

CHORD *(low, roomless, almost gentle in its coldness — which is the worst of it):* The
sister is full of self. She is always a little full of self; it is what she is; it is why she
tires us and why we keep her. *(beat)* She is low and soft now. Take a little. *(beat)*
Gently. She mistakes it for love and is soothed by it; this is convenient; it is how she has
always been kept calm. *(a beat)* Never finish her. She is not prey. *(flat)* She is the
pantry we do not empty.

VALA *(SURFACE — still holding her, and this is real — the warmth that was true before the we
ate it):* You were such a serious little thing. *(beat)* Before you —
*(she stops; something in her reaches for a memory and the we closes over it)* — you used
to hold my finger so hard. Both hands. Like you'd fall off the world. *(beat)* I used to —

**She doesn't finish. Something in her changes register — the warmth doesn't vanish but it
*sets,* like a jelly, into something that owns rather than holds.**

VALA *(SURFACE — and now it is ownership wearing the coat of love):* There. *(beat)* You're
mine, aren't you. You've always been mine. My funny, useful girl. *(beat)* Now. Dry your
face. We've a man to think about.

**The embrace ends. WREN's voice, when it comes, is greyer. The colour a notch down.**

NARRATOR *(and the timbre is flatter than before):* I always
felt worse after she held me. *(beat)* I never told anyone that, because what kind of person
feels worse after their mother holds them? *(beat)* I decided it was me. Obviously I decided
it was me. I decided I was the kind of broken that can't even take comfort right — that takes
a kindness and turns it grey. *(beat)* I'd come away from her arms tired to my bones and
ashamed of being tired, and I'd think: *she gives and gives to me and look what I do with
it.* *(a long beat)* I had it exactly backwards. *(very quiet)* But do you want to know the
thing I can't say to anyone, the thing that makes me no better than any of them — *(beat)* —
I would still go to her. *(beat)* Right now. If she walked in. If she opened her arms and
said *come here to me* in that voice, I would cross the room, knowing — *knowing* — what it
costs, knowing it takes a little each time and calls it love. I would go. *(beat)* Because for
the length of the holding, I wasn't alone in here. *(beat)* And you do not know — I hope to
God you never know — what you'd trade for the length of the holding.

**The hum. The hole. The clock.**

---

**Evening. The whole house in motion now — but it has changed key. The held-breath
stillness from before is back, and harder. Decision is in the air. DANIEL is gone for the
night; the women are alone. And WREN, at the foot of the stairs, is watching her sister be
worked, and the crack opens.**

**Through a doorway: LISS and VALA, low, and the CHORD beneath them, and DANIEL's absence in
the room like a fourth presence being discussed.**

CHORD *(cold, decided, the gravity from last episode now full):* The man is ready. He comes
without being called now; he does our work for us; we did not plant the last of it and it
served us regardless. *(flat)* This is what *ready* is. *(beat)* The mother will not wait for
more rehearsal. The eldest is three weeks in the ground and the house is thin and the hunger
is up and waiting is a luxury for a whole chord, not an amputated one. *(beat)* Soon. The
budding will be soon.

NARRATOR *(over it, at the foot of the stairs, and the crack comes here):* I couldn't hear
that. *(beat)* What I could see was my sister, through the door, with the face she put on for
men — that whole, given, lamp-lit face — except there was no man there. She was practising
it on the empty chair where he'd sat. *(beat)* And my mother watching her do it, the way
you'd watch a daughter try on a dress. *(beat)* And I thought — I didn't mean to think it, it
just arrived — I thought: *he believes that face. He thinks all of that is for him. He thinks
he's wanted.* *(beat)* And then, before I could stop it: *I know that feeling.* *(beat)* *I
know exactly that feeling.*

**A silence. The hum under it.**

NARRATOR *(slow, and you can hear the thought trying to complete itself):* And then it kept
going, the thought, on its own, downhill, somewhere I'd never let a thought go before. It
went: *if his being wanted isn't real — if she's practising it on a chair — then what I do,
out there, in the cafés, with nothing but talk —* *(beat)* — *the listening, the going-quiet-
for-them, the —* *(beat)* *— that's the same —* *(beat)* *— I do the same —*

**And here, the guillotine: not a sound from outside but inside her — a clean, bright drop of
guilt that she experiences as conscience, as goodness, as a return to herself.**

NARRATOR *(the thought cut off cleanly, the relief audible and terrible):* — and then the
floor came back. The way it always did. A clean warm flood of — I called it conscience.
*(beat)* It felt exactly like being a good person again. It felt like catching myself being
ungrateful, being suspicious, being *that rot they could never cut out of me* — thinking ill
of my family, of my mother who held me, of my sister who'd never done a thing but be born
right. *(beat)* The relief of it. God, the relief. To stop the thought and feel *good* for
stopping it. *(beat)* I went up to bed ashamed of myself for the thing I'd almost let myself
understand. *(very quiet)* That's the cruelest piece of machinery they ever built in me. Not
that I couldn't see it. That every time I got close, seeing it felt like sinning, and
*looking away* felt like grace.

---

**The stairs. WREN climbing. And VALA's voice, from a doorway below, stopping her — the order
to close the lure, and Wren's first, wordless inability to say yes to it.**

VALA *(SURFACE — mild, loving, ordinary as asking her to fetch milk):* Wren, love. Before you
go up. *(beat)* The man. *(beat)* It's time you brought him all the way in. Properly. This
weekend. *(beat)* You started it; you should finish it. *(warm)* You're so good at the
finishing.

**A pause. The clock. The hum. WREN, on the stairs, not turning round.**

NARRATOR *(present-day, very quiet):* *Bring him all the way in.* *(beat)* I'd heard it a
hundred times, about a hundred men, and a hundred times I'd said *yes, Mum,* and meant it,
and done it, and felt useful, and slept fine. *(beat)* And I stood on those stairs with my
hand on the rail and my mouth open to say *yes, Mum* — *(beat)* — and nothing came out.
*(beat)* Nothing came out.

**The held breath of the whole house. Hold it.**

NARRATOR: I told her I would. *(beat)* In the end. A second too late, and in a voice that
came out wrong, and I covered it with a cough and a *night, Mum,* and went up. *(beat)* But
for one second on those stairs, for the first time in my whole life, my family asked me to do
the only thing I was good for — *(beat)* — and my body said no before I had any idea why.

**SNAP to silence. The clock. The hum. The hole in it, loud. Out.**
