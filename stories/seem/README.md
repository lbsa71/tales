# SEEM

### A radio play / prestige literary-horror audio drama · a season of 7 half-hour episodes

> A rejected daughter of a family that shares one mind is sent to lure the men they
> need — until she lingers too long with the one man she was supposed to deliver, sees
> him too clearly, and her first act of mercy destroys the only people who were ever hers.

This folder holds the **manuscript** — seven episode scripts in `chapters/`. The
engine and rules live in [`story-bible.md`](./story-bible.md); the sell, the
proof-of-concept opening, and the misread-guardrails live in
[`pitch-packet.md`](./pitch-packet.md). Read those two first. This README only records
what the manuscript *decided* and how to read the page.

---

## THE TITLE

**SEEM.** It only ever *seems.* The warmth the man falls into is real to him and made
for him; he is right about the feeling and wrong about everything else. And under the
homophone is the device itself — the **seam** between what the chord plants and what the
man then feels, the seam you can hear, the seam that isn't there inside the *we* "because
there is no seam in the we." (Makers only. Never explain the pun in public copy.)

## HOW TO READ THE PAGE — the three-stream format key

The whole form is three kinds of listening at once (bible §8). On the page:

- **Bold paragraphs** = sound design / stage direction — room tone, rain, music, the
  shape of a silence. The bold is the sound; treat it as a cue, not flavour.
- `NARRATOR:` — **Wren**, our protagonist, years later, *reconstructing.* Intimate, dry,
  self-deprecating, close as a confession. She **cannot hear the CHORD** — not then, not
  now. When her narration drifts near it, she is *imagining* into a room she was always
  shut out of. She is unreliable in **one direction only**: she undervalues herself.
- `(SURFACE)` — spoken aloud, in the room. Fully naturalistic, real acoustics, warm.
  Deliberately bland. Where the listener feels safe.
- `(CHORD)` — the channel. **Not** narration. Layered near-unison voices, close-mic'd,
  **no room reverb at all** (it is not in the room; it is inside a skull). Cold,
  appraising, procedural. It calls the Maiden **"the woman" / "the daughter"** — never
  her name, never "her." **One voice short:** the eldest is newly dead, so there is an
  audible *gap* in the chord. Do not fill it. Let it ache.
- `(HIM)` — **Daniel's** interior. A single warm, fully-roomed voice — a real self. The
  horror is **delay and disguise:** the CHORD plants something; a beat — or a whole scene —
  later, HIM blooms it as his own, with total conviction. **Vary the lag** so it never
  goes metronomic; sometimes let him resist and hear the channel adjust; sometimes give
  him a true thought the chord didn't plant that happens to serve them anyway. **His one
  seam-free thread is his feeling for Wren** — no CHORD line ever precedes it. The
  listener can hear it is the real one, and cannot tell her.

**Who hears what — the dramatic-irony engine.** The **listener** hears all of it. **Daniel**
hears only `(SURFACE)` + his own `(HIM)`. **Wren** hears only `(SURFACE)`. The listener
holds the truth the two people capable of love are denied.

## THE CAST (names chosen for the manuscript)

- **Wren** — the Failed Sister. Narrator. The dud the family keeps. The only one who can
  love. (Her name is the plain, small, outside-sounding one — the only real person in the
  house.)
- **Liss** — the Maiden. The current vessel, under-trained, going through the budding.
- **Vala** — the Mother. Never a villain. Lear, not a monster. *(völva — buried, never said.)*
- **Edda** — the Crone. **Dead before the first scene.** Her absence is the gap in the
  chord. *(In Old Norse, "edda" means great-grandmother. Buried, never said.)*
- **Daniel** — the Man. The most human-feeling character because he is the only ordinary
  human. The work's one true love, and its honest cost.

## THE [OPEN] FORKS — decided (bible §13)

The bible left nine tuning forks open and made the manuscript decide them. It did:

1. **The Man's ending → LIVING WOUND.** Daniel survives, hollowed — colours turned down —
   and walks back out into a world that will never know what he was, never knowing himself
   what she felt. Colder and truer to how this using works; keeps him an unresolved cost
   so the consolation can't ring hypocritical (§11). She can never reach him, repair him,
   or be known by him. (The narration is therefore not literal elegy but something worse:
   reconstructing a man who is still alive and unreachable.)
2. **Bonding switch → CREEPING / ratcheting.** Every outside father reintroduces active
   hardware; suppression gets harder each generation; the line has been losing ground for
   a long time. *This* generation — a kept dud who can love **and** a Maiden who catches
   the bond — is the dam breaking. The chord is a doomed evolutionary strategy finally
   collapsing, so "the chord dies" (§11) is earned by biology, not one bad night.
3. **Caregiving warmth toward infants → ON, then curdles.** Vala felt genuine mammalian
   warmth toward the infant Liss — and, the dagger, toward the infant Wren — before the
   link came online and swallowed it into ownership. Warmer, sadder, more Lear. It is the
   half-remembered warmth Wren has spent her life trying to earn back ("she would still go
   back," §5).
4. **Individuation ignites → DURING / AFTER the man is used.** The budding completes; the
   man is used anyway; her stall only delayed it. Pure grief with nothing to do — no
   thriller-shaped hope to crush.
5. **The opening → SILENCE → LURE.** A breath of the amputated household, then the
   proof-of-concept lure (pitch packet §2), near-verbatim, as the spine of Episode 1.
6. **Chord self-awareness of the witch-history → THEY KNOW.** Deep-time memory; they have
   moved many times; they do not like fire. Seeded as stray fact and atmosphere only —
   **never named.**
7. **Courtship duration → SHORT COURTSHIP (days/weeks).** Gives Daniel reality and deepens
   the betrayal of the hand-off.
8. **Kept duds → SHE IS THE ONLY ONE.** Expelled failed-daughters of past generations
   exist as distant, unexploited texture (others out there who never knew what they were).
9. **Format → 7 half-hour episodes.** Enough to build the pattern the dread depends on,
   without sag.

## EPISODE INDEX

1. **The Wrong Kind of Man** — the lure; the amputated house; the seam, heard.
2. **Reps** — Daniel returns; a colder, older pattern shown plainly; a man with his colours turned down.
3. **The Hard Week** — the courtship deepens; the crack: *he thinks he's wanted; I know that feeling.*
4. **Step Back** — the stall; the smallest, most cowardly mercy there is.
5. **The Budding** — grief overrides patience; the man is used; a single "I" tears loose.
6. **A Self** — the disease takes; the chord turns on itself; the concept finally lands.
7. **Across the Gap** — the chord dies, and is mourned; two sisters reach across the gap; the man is carried out, unresolved.

## THE REGRESSION TEST

Bible **Appendix A** is the north star: if a scene can no longer earn one of those raves,
it has drifted, and the annotation says which section to return to. The per-episode map in
[`SERIES_OUTLINE.md`](./SERIES_OUTLINE.md) tags each beat to the bible section and the rave
it is written to deserve.

The two things that had to survive contact with the page: **the device** — you can hear a
self being born — and **the belief underneath it** — what she was taught was her brokenness
is her capacity to love. Everything else serves those.
