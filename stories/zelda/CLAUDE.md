# CLAUDE.md - AI Assistant Guide for "A Rabbit's Life"

## Project Overview

**Project**: "A Rabbit's Life" - A hyperrealistic, non-anthropomorphic novella
**Narrative Perspective**: First-person narration from a domestic rabbit's sensory experience
**Current Status**: First draft complete (v1.2, ~12,900 words, 10 chapters), entering revision cycle
**Core Challenge**: Maintain absolute biological accuracy and non-anthropomorphic voice throughout

**Central Question**: "If I choose to take a sentient being from its mother, deny its instincts, control its entire existence, and end its life without it ever knowing freedom, what immense responsibility do I bear to make that life as rich as possible?"

## Critical Understanding

This is **NOT** a whimsical animal story. This is a rigorous literary experiment using clinical precision to confront readers with the ethical implications of pet ownership. The rabbit narrator cannot think, wonder, hope, or understand in human terms - only experience through scent, sound, temperature, touch, and bodily states.

## Vital Constraints

### Absolute Prohibitions

**NEVER use these constructions**:
- Emotional attribution: "I think", "I feel", "I wonder", "I hope", "I miss", "I love", "I want", "I wish"
- Metaphor/simile: "as if", "like a", "reminds me of"
- Speculation: "must have", "seemed to", "appeared to"
- Understanding: "I realize", "I understand", "I know that", "I remember when"
- Narrative reflection: "looking back", "now I know"
- Time concepts: "yesterday", "tomorrow", "last week", "years ago"
- Death concepts: "dead", "dying", "death", "forever gone"
- Abstract human concepts: "home", "love", "happiness", "sadness", "loneliness"
- Em-dashes or en-dashes for subordinate clauses

**NEVER attribute to the rabbit**:
- Understanding of human language beyond tone/sound
- Knowledge of captivity vs. freedom
- Concept of time beyond light cycles
- Understanding of death
- Theory of mind (knowing what others think)
- Planning beyond immediate instinct

### Required Approaches

**ALWAYS express emotions through**:
- Physical sensations: "the body trembles", "heat floods the muscles"
- Behavioral manifestations: "the body presses against her flank"
- Sensory states: "the scent is wrong", "the sound is sharp"
- Bodily states: "hunger", "the breathing quickens", "the heart strikes fast"

**ALWAYS ground descriptions in**:
- Scent (primary sense for rabbits)
- Sound and vibration
- Temperature
- Light and shadow
- Touch and texture
- Body position and movement
- Hunger, thirst, pain, comfort

**ALWAYS use biologically accurate**:
- Sensory hierarchy (scent > hearing > vision)
- Behavioral patterns (freezing, thumping, grooming, nesting)
- Social dynamics (hierarchy, territorial behavior)
- Reproductive cycles and cancer progression
- Lifespan and aging markers
- Prey animal responses

### Terminology Standards

**Rabbits**:
- Companion rabbit: "sister" (PRIMARY), "nest-sister", "the nester"
- Self: "the body"
- Other rabbits: descriptive + "body" or "rabbit"

**Humans**:
- General: "tall-body" or "tall-bodies" (PRIMARY)
- Specific: "smaller tall-body" (child), "larger tall-body" (adult)
- NEVER: "owner", "human", "person", "caretaker"

**Spatial**:
- Enclosure: "the enclosure", "the space"
- NEVER: "cage", "home", "room"

### Rhythm and Pacing by Chapter

- **Chapters 1-3 (Birth/Youth)**: Mix short and medium sentences, connective tissue, lyrical energy
- **Chapters 4-7 (Middle Years)**: Rhythmic, meditative, routine shown through variation
- **Chapters 8-9 (Illness/Death)**: Deliberate, weighted sentences, white space for impact
- **Chapter 10 (Alone)**: Gradual tense shift (present→past), sentences simplifying to fragments

## Critical Success Factors

### This project succeeds when:
- Readers experience the story through pure rabbit sensory perception
- The ethical confrontation emerges naturally from immersion, not preaching
- Biological accuracy creates authenticity
- Restraint creates emotional power
- The voice never breaks

### This project fails when:
- Narrator "thinks" or "understands" in human terms
- Metaphors distance reader from direct experience
- Biological inaccuracies break immersion
- Sentimentality replaces sensory precision
- The ethical question is stated rather than shown

## Development Philosophy

**Clinical precision + Poetic rhythm + Devastating restraint = Emotional power**

The most powerful moments come from **what is NOT said**. The rabbit's inability to comprehend death, separation, or time makes these experiences more devastating, not less.

**Trust the constraints.** The non-anthropomorphic rules are not limitations - they are the source of the work's unique power.

## Emergency Reference

If ever uncertain whether something breaks the voice, ask:

1. Could this sentence appear in a veterinary textbook observation of rabbit behavior?
2. Am I attributing human cognition to the rabbit?
3. Is this sensory experience biologically possible for *Oryctolagus cuniculus*?
4. Does this express emotion through body/behavior rather than internal state?
5. Would removing metaphor/speculation make this stronger?

**When in doubt, make it more sensory, more physical, more restrained.**
