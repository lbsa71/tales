## Phase 2.5: Short Story Condensation (Optional)

### Objective
Transform completed novella draft into short story format (7,000-8,000 words) while preserving all emotional impact and narrative coherence.

### Philosophy: Compression as Intensification
The shorter format makes the story MORE intense, not less. Every word works harder. Protected emotional peaks stand out more dramatically against compressed surroundings.

### Three-Phase Approach

**Phase 1 - Aggressive Condensation (45-53% reduction)**
- Chapters 1-4: Cut repetitive sensory catalogs, consolidate sequences
- Target: 2,300-2,500 words cut
- Technique: Merge repetitions, imply patterns rather than enumerating

**Phase 2 - Moderate Condensation (31-41% reduction)**
- Chapters 6, 7, 9: Tighten around protected scenes
- Target: 1,400 words cut
- Technique: Protect key emotional beats, compress transitions

**Phase 3 - Minimal Condensation (21-26% reduction)**
- Chapters 5, 8, 10: EMOTIONAL CORES - surgical precision only
- Target: 1,000-1,100 words cut
- Protected scenes (DO NOT SHORTEN):
  - Ch 5: Nesting ritual, fur-pulling, "Nothing comes"
  - Ch 8: Scent fading from hollow
  - Ch 10: Death sequence, "What was one facet closes"

### Condensation Techniques
1. **Density over Elaboration**: One perfect example instead of three
2. **Implication over Enumeration**: Show once, then reference
3. **Efficient Language**: Verbs carrying multiple meanings
4. **Protected Peaks**: Sacred scenes at full power
5. **Strategic White Space**: Section breaks carry weight without words

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Anthropomorphic Slips
- Using "feel" for emotions (allowed only for physical sensation)
- Having rabbits "understand" anything conceptual
- Writing "she seemed to" or "as if she knew"
- **Solution**: Always ask "Could a rabbit experience this?"

### Rhythm Problems
- Strings of two-word fragments (becomes choppy, sacrifices readability)
- All long flowing sentences (loses immediacy and urgency)
- Uniform sentence length throughout (monotonous, loses emotional texture)
- **Solution**: Vary sentence complexity to serve the moment. Use fragments sparingly for emphasis. Allow connective tissue and flowing observation. Match rhythm to chapter position per Style Guide.

### Emotional Manipulation
- Adding pathos not earned by narrative
- Telling reader how to feel
- **Solution**: Show only, let reader respond

### Inconsistent Motifs
- Hawthorn appearing in wrong chapters
- Scent descriptions inconsistent
- **Solution**: Check Theme Tracker before writing each scene
