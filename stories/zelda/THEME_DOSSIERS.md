# Theme Dossiers / Stage-Specific Notes

## Arc Progress Dossier: Micro-Scenes by Chapter

## Chapter 1: Birth

**Timeline**: Days 1-21 (Birth through Week 3)
**Developmental Stage**: Neonatal to early development - confined to nest box, eyes opening, milk exclusively

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Touch (warmth, pressure, body contact, pile dynamics)
- **Secondary**: Scent (milk, mother, litter-mates, differentiation)
- **Tertiary**: Sound (heartbeats plural, breathing rhythms, voices)
- **Absent**: Light (darkness of nest initially), Cold (warmth dominates)
- **Emerging Late**: Light (eyes open days 10-12), Individual scent recognition

### Bodily State
- Full (milk), warm, safe, compressed
- **Many bodies initially** (litter of 3-4), then two after selection
- Movement limited initially, then growing mobility within nest box
- Eyes opening toward middle of chapter
- World is touch-radius, expanding gradually
- Pile dynamics: warmth-seeking, center-pressing, huddling

### Environmental Details
- Nest box interior: wood walls, straw bedding, wood shavings
- No earth beneath, only the pull toward it
- Enclosed, dark initially, then light filtering in
- **Confined to nest box** - not yet moved to hutch proper
- Space feels larger after litter-mates removed

### Behavioral Beats
- **Litter experience**: Pile breathing together, many heartbeats
- Nursing: mother visits briefly, leaves, kits huddle
- Mother's pattern: periodic visits (1-2x daily), mostly absent
- Eyes opening: world differentiating, "this body, that body, another body"
- Scent recognition: one particular scent becoming familiar (future sister)
- Mother's scent fading over time, nursing less frequent
- **Selection event**: Hands descending, bodies lifted, scents disappearing
- **Siblings removed**: Scent trails ending, pile diminishing, two remain
- Sister-bond solidification: "the one who remained"
- First movements: legs finding use, exploring nest box limits
- Scratching at wood (instinct to burrow)
- Human handling: both kits lifted briefly

### Human Presence
- Absent or muffled initially
- Vibrations through wood occasionally
- **Selection scene**: Multiple tall-bodies, hands entering nest box
- Bodies lifted and removed (siblings taken away)
- Narrator and sister both lifted, then placed back
- Voices carrying vibration into nest box
- Not understood as beings, only sensory experience

### Thematic Resonance
- Birth into captivity already complete
- **Separation from litter**: First major loss, siblings taken
- **Sister-bond earned**: Not arbitrary pairing, but shared survival
- Burrow-longing present from first moment
- **Many to two**: Pile warmth lost, compensating with sister
- Mother's gradual absence (biological norm, not abandonment)
- Growing awareness of confinement
- **Ethical weight**: Kits separated very young (week 3)

### Chapter 1 Ending
- Eyes fully open
- **Two bodies only**, sister-bond established
- Awareness of nest box boundaries
- Pull toward earth intensifying
- Anticipation of "outside" the nest box (the hutch proper)
- **Does NOT include**: Solid food, pellets, hay, full mobility in larger space

## Chapter 2: First World

**Timeline**: Days 22-35 (Weeks 4-5)  
**Developmental Stage**: Weaning stage - moved from nest box to hutch proper, first solid foods, establishing hierarchy

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (new environment, wood, straw, **first hay and pellets**)
- **Secondary**: Taste (first solid food - hay, pellets)
- **Tertiary**: Sound (gate, footsteps, new sounds in larger hutch)
- **New**: Space (larger enclosure, more mobility)

### Bodily State
- Growing, curious, fully mobile
- **First solid food, new tastes** (weaning in progress)
- Learning body's capabilities
- Coordination improved, confident hopping
- Belly fills differently (solid vs milk)

### Environmental Details
- **Hutch proper** (moved from nest box): feeding area, sleeping area
- Wood floor, straw scattered
- Hay rack mounted on wall
- Metal dishes for pellets and water
- More space than nest box (multiple hops corner to corner)
- View of enclosure boundaries
- Gaps between boards showing outside

### Behavioral Beats
- **Move from nest box to hutch** - the "square opens wider"
- **First encounter with solid food**: Pellets in metal dish, hay in rack
- First eating order established (dominance)
- Chewing rhythm different from nursing
- Mounting behavior begins (empty instinct)
- Exploration of hutch limits
- First gate sound → food arrival association
- Scratching at gaps between boards (earth scent beyond)

### Human Presence
- Gate sound precedes arrival
- Large shapes, unfamiliar scent
- Hand placing food dishes, filling hay rack, replacing water
- **Daily feeding routine establishes**
- Not threatening, not understood
- Bodies freeze when humans approach, then relax when they leave

### Thematic Resonance
- Captivity's rhythm begins (gate-food-gate pattern)
- Dominance without social meaning (feeding order)
- World's limits established in larger space
- First tastes of solid food mark transition from infancy
- Weaning = first loss (mother's milk gone, sister remains)

### Chapter 2 Ending
- Routine established but pull toward outside intensifies
- Earth scent through gaps between boards
- Gate opens one day and **stays open** (not just for feeding)
- **First earth contact** - moved from hutch to outdoor yard area
- Bodies on earth for the first time

## Chapter 3: First Earth

**Timeline**: Days 36-56+ (Weeks 6-8+)  
**Developmental Stage**: Fully weaned, juvenile stage, adult behaviors emerging

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (earth, grass, wind, rain)
- **Secondary**: Touch (soil, grass texture, wind on fur)
- **Tertiary**: Sound (wind in hawthorn, outdoor sounds)
- **New**: Temperature variation (sun, shade, cold)

### Bodily State
- Alert, excited, overwhelmed
- First outdoor exposure
- Digging impulse frustrated (ground too hard)
- Brief escape: exhilaration, then fear, then return
- Post-return: settling back into familiar

### Environmental Details
- Yard with earth floor, wire boundary at hutch's edge
- Hawthorn tree in corner
- Weather: spring, variable
- Gap in wire leading to yard and woods beyond

### Behavioral Beats
- First contact with earth (pause, scent)
- Attempted digging (fails, ground packed)
- Discovery of hawthorn tree, roots, hollow beginning
- First rain on fur
- Brief escape through gap in wire
- Night in woods, predator scent, terror
- Return to familiar
- "No hole. Wall. More wall." – reinforced wire

### Human Presence
- Gate sound to outdoor area
- Watched from distance
- Voices carry differently outdoors
- Reinforcement after escape discovered

### Thematic Resonance
- Earth contact: what was longed for
- Immediate limitation: cannot dig proper burrow
- Hawthorn tree becomes center point
- Freedom briefly tasted, then denied
- Captivity made certain

## Chapter 4: Seasons

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Temperature (seasonal changes)
- **Secondary**: Scent (seasonal shifts in vegetation)
- **Tertiary**: Light (day length changes)

### Bodily State
- Settled, routine-following
- Body adjusting to seasonal cycles
- Molt patterns, fur thickness changes

### Environmental Details
- Summer: dry earth, long light
- Autumn: damp, falling leaves, shortening days
- Winter: cold, frost, limited outdoor time
- Spring: return of warmth, new growth

### Behavioral Beats
- Routine solidifying: gate → food → outdoor time
- Smaller sister begins escape attempts
- Larger sister stays, less adventurous
- Mounting behavior continues, meaningless

### Human Presence
- Routine established: morning gate, evening gate
- Voices change pitch over time (children aging)
- Handling for health checks

### Thematic Resonance
- Time passing without being counted
- Captivity normalized through repetition
- First hints of human puberty

## Chapter 5: Nesting

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (hay, belly fur, earth in hollow)
- **Secondary**: Touch (fur-pulling sensation, hay in mouth)
- **Tertiary**: Temperature (ground cool, body warm)

### Bodily State
- Restless, driven, hormonal
- Pulling sensation at belly
- Rapid breathing, compulsive behavior

### Environmental Details
- Spring, new growth
- Hawthorn hollow
- Damp earth, fresh grass

### Behavioral Beats
- Carrying hay in mouth to hollow
- Circling, arranging, re-arranging
- Pulling fur from belly (pain/relief)
- Waiting in nest
- Nothing comes

### Human Presence
- Observed from distance
- Extra hay placed (noticed, used)
- Voice sounds, gate sounds continue

### Thematic Resonance
- **Peak of unfulfilled instinct**
- Biological program completing, biological purpose absent
- Most devastating scene: instinct without meaning

## Chapter 6: Middle Years

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (human scent changes, familiar routine scents)
- **Secondary**: Sound (voices deepening, changing)
- **Tertiary**: All senses routine, unremarkable

### Bodily State
- Mature, stable, routine
- Seasonal cycles familiar
- Bodies fully grown

### Environmental Details
- Same environment, years passing
- Hawthorn tree larger

### Behavioral Beats
- Larger sister nests annually, nothing comes
- Eating order maintained
- Human puberty observed through scent
- Predator (cat) arrives, confrontation established

### Human Presence
- Voices change (children → adolescents)
- Scent changes (puberty)
- Movement patterns change (growth)
- Still bring food, still open gate

### The Cat
- New scent arrives: musty, sharp (predator markers)
- Small kitten drops into yard (jumps over low fence)
- Smaller sister responds with aggression, not flight
- Foot-thumping, lunging forward (no wire between them)
- Kitten scrambles back over fence
- Pattern repeats as kitten grows: drops in, driven back, learns
- Adult cat respects boundary: lies beyond fence, watching, tail twitching
- Humor element: predator conditioned to fear prey

### Thematic Resonance
- Years compressed into routine
- Parallel development: rabbits aging, humans aging
- Captivity fully normalized
- External threat, managed through dominance

## Chapter 7: Change

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (iron, sweetness, illness)
- **Secondary**: Touch (belly swollen, body changing)
- **Tertiary**: Sound (breathing heavier, movement slower)

### Bodily State
- One body changing (larger sister)
- Swelling, heaviness, fatigue
- Other body noticing difference

### Environmental Details
- Same space, different dynamic
- Hollow used for rest, not nesting
- Role reversal beginning

### Behavioral Beats
- Larger sister eating less, moving less
- Smaller sister approaching food first
- Roles reversing
- First vet visit (metal, antiseptic, shaved fur)

### Human Presence
- Different handling (more gentle, more frequent)
- Carrier box, car vibration
- Strange place (vet): metal surfaces, chemical scents
- Return to familiar, but scent changed

### Thematic Resonance
- Body as messenger of mortality
- Medical intervention without comprehension
- Dominance shifting through illness

## Chapter 8: Absence

**Timeline**: Early autumn, sister's death
**Time from birth**: ~7 years

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (fading), absence of scent
- **Secondary**: Touch (cold body when returned)
- **Tertiary**: Sound (human weeping, strange vocalization)

### Bodily State
- Survivor: alert, seeking
- Sister: taken, then returned cold, still

### Environmental Details
- Hollow empty
- Scent fading from straw
- Space where warmth was

### Behavioral Beats
- Taken away (carrier box, car)
- Waiting, scent weakening in space
- Body returned: wrapped in cloth, cold, no response
- Nosing the body, no scent-response
- Circling, returning to body, no change

### Human Presence
- Carrying body back
- Salt-water scent (tears)
- Shaking bodies, strange sounds (weeping)
- Placing body near survivor
- Taking body away again

### Thematic Resonance
- Loss as physical absence, not concept
- Body-return as incomprehensible event
- Human grief observed without understanding

## Chapter 9: Solitude

**Timeline**: First winter alone → into second year
**Time since sister's death**: Weeks to ~1 year

### Sensory Palette
- **Primary**: Scent (fading, memory-scent)
- **Secondary**: Sound (wind through hawthorn, sounds like breathing)
- **Tertiary**: Touch (empty space where warmth was)

### Bodily State
- Alone for first time
- Eating less, moving less
- Seeking behavior, finding nothing
- Aging: "simply old, simply alone"

### Environmental Details
- Same space, half-empty
- Hollow holds fading scent
- Wind through hawthorn constant

### Behavioral Beats
- Returning to hollow repeatedly
- Nosing straw where sister lay
- Reduced eating, listlessness

### The Cat Confrontation
- Cat returns, senses the change in rabbit's behavior
- Cat respects fence boundary (learned from years of encounters)
- Hutch door left open - cat enters wooden enclosure
- Inside the ultimate safe space: straw, darkness, no escape
- The body that once defended cannot rise
- Fierceness required someone to protect
- Human intervention: cat flees, door latched
- Horror intensified: not yard invasion but hutch violation

### Human Presence
- Gate sounds continue
- Food still placed
- More handling (checking health)
- Voices quieter
- Eventual intervention during cat confrontation

### Thematic Resonance
- Loss experienced bodily
- Scent as only form of "memory"
- Wind-sound as phantom presence
- The same body that defended fiercely can no longer defend alone

## Chapter 10: Return

**Timeline**: Second winter (survivor's death)
**Time since sister's death**: ~1-2 years
**Total lifespan**: ~8-9 years

### Sensory Palette
- **All senses present, then fading**
- Scent: earth, air, fading all
- Sound: wind, then quieting
- Touch: cold, then nothing
- Light: dimming

### Bodily State
- Final winter, body failing
- Heartbeat slowing
- Breathing shallow
- Warmth leaving
- Sister's scent now "almost imagined" (nearly gone from hollow)

### Environmental Details
- Winter: cold, short light
- Hollow beneath hawthorn
- Earth finally receiving

### Behavioral Beats
- Long rests, little movement
- Final return to hollow
- Body stilling
- Senses dissolving

### Human Presence
- Gate sounds continue until they don't
- Final handling or simply fading: narrative choice to be made
- Not central to this chapter

### Thematic Resonance
- Death as physical process, not event
- Earth receiving what was always longed for
- Facet closing, individual merging with all
- Tense shift: past → present → dissolution
