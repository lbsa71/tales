## Detailed Motif Analysis

### Hawthorn Tree
**First appearance**: Chapter 3 (First spring outdoors)
**Physical description**: Tree in corner of yard, roots creating hollow beneath
**Sensory associations**: Bark scent, leaf rustle, root-coolness, wind-sound
**Symbolic function**: 
- Center of outdoor territory
- Place where larger sister nests
- Where survivor returns after loss
- Final resting place

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 3: Discovery, first digging attempt near roots
- Ch. 5: Larger sister builds nest in hollow beneath
- Ch. 6: Regular resting spot during middle years
- Ch. 9: Survivor returns to hollow after body's return
- Ch. 10: Final location, wind through branches sounds like sister

### Burrow-longing
**First appearance**: Chapter 1 (Birth)
**Physical description**: Pull toward earth, instinct to dig down, to be enclosed
**Sensory associations**: Earth-scent, darkness, enclosure-safety, coolness
**Symbolic function**:
- What captivity denies absolutely
- Instinct present from birth without possibility
- Represents gap between biology and environment

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 1: "No earth. Only the pull toward it."
- Ch. 3: First contact with soil, digging that yields nothing (ground too hard)
- Ch. 5: Nesting as displaced burrow-behavior
- Ch. 10: Death as finally being received by earth

### Scent Memory
**First appearance**: Chapter 1 (Mother's scent)
**Physical description**: How past persists in present, through scent-associations
**Sensory associations**: Specific scent notes linked to beings, places, states
**Symbolic function**:
- Only form of "memory" the narrator can have
- Loss experienced as scent fading
- Past present in body, not in concept

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 1: Mother's scent fading after separation
- Ch. 8: Sister's scent changing (illness)
- Ch. 9: Scent fading from hollow, from straw
- Ch. 10: "The body holds": scent memories as final persistence

### Gate Sound
**First appearance**: Chapter 2 (Move to new hutch)
**Physical description**: Metal latch, wooden frame, specific vibration pattern
**Sensory associations**: Click, creak, footstep-sequence following
**Symbolic function**:
- Marker of captivity's rhythm
- Human arrival always preceded by same sound
- Routine made audible

**Recurrences**: Throughout all chapters as constant
- Marks feeding time
- Marks human attention/handling
- Marks vet visits (same gate, different outcome)
- Absence of gate sound = isolation

### Earth/Soil Contact
**First appearance**: Chapter 3 (First outdoor spring)
**Physical description**: Actual earth beneath feet rather than wood/straw
**Sensory associations**: Dampness, coolness, mineral scent, diggability
**Symbolic function**:
- What was always denied
- Brief contact with natural substrate
- What receives body in death

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 3: First contact, first failed digging
- Ch. 5: Earth beneath nest area
- Ch. 10: "Earth receiving": death as return

### Nesting Behavior
**First appearance**: Chapter 5 (First spring nesting)
**Physical description**: Carrying hay, pulling belly fur, circling, arranging
**Sensory associations**: Hay scent, fur-pulling sensation, restlessness
**Symbolic function**:
- Peak of unfulfilled instinct
- Biological program firing without biological possibility
- Most emotionally intense moment

**Note**: Does not recur, shown once, with full weight

### Human Scent Changes
**First appearance**: Chapter 6 (Middle years)
**Physical description**: Puberty observed through olfaction
**Sensory associations**: Muskier, sharper, sweat-different
**Symbolic function**:
- Time passing marked bodily
- Rabbits observe human development without understanding
- Parallel aging processes

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 6: First scent changes in younger human
- Ch. 7: Both humans fully changed, adult scent

### Metal/Antiseptic Smell
**First appearance**: Chapter 7 (First vet visit)
**Physical description**: Veterinary clinic sensory environment
**Sensory associations**: Sharp chemical smell, cold metal surfaces, strange hands
**Symbolic function**:
- Medical intervention without comprehension
- Approaching death marked by this scent
- Alien environment, maximum disorientation

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 7: First illness, first vet visit
- Ch. 8: Repeat visits, scent now associated with sister's decline

### Wire Breach / Escape
**First appearance**: Chapter 3 (First spring/summer outdoors)
**Physical description**: Gap in wire, passage to yard and woods beyond
**Sensory associations**: Wire scraping on fur, overwhelming forest scents
**Symbolic function**:
- Brief taste of freedom that emphasizes captivity
- Exhilaration followed by terror
- Human power to close what was briefly opened

### The Cat (Predator)
**First appearance**: Chapter 6 (Middle years, as kitten)
**Physical description**: Small kitten with orange and white patches → full-grown adult with thick fur
**Sensory associations**: Musty, ammonia-tinged, predator markers
**Symbolic function**:
- External threat introducing fear into captive routine
- Kitten conditioned to fear rabbits through repeated defensive encounters
- Adult cat respects learned boundary (humor: predator intimidated by prey)
- After companion's death: same rabbit can no longer defend when cat enters hutch

**Recurrences**:
- Ch. 6: Kitten drops into yard, smaller sister drives it back; pattern repeats as kitten grows
- Ch. 6: Adult cat watches from beyond fence, respects boundary
- Ch. 9: Cat enters hutch (door left open); surviving smaller sister cannot defend

### Wall Reinforced
**First appearance**: Chapter 3 (After escape)
**Physical description**: Humans repair the wire, add reinforcement
**Sensory associations**: New wire smell, familiar routes blocked
**Symbolic function**:
- Human response to rabbit agency
- Freedom attempted and denied
- "No hole. Wall. More wall." – captivity's totality

## Thematic Threads

### Thread 1: Unfulfilled Instinct
**Progression**: Burrow-longing → Failed digging → Nesting → Death as resolution
**Question posed**: What does it mean to have biological drives that can never be satisfied?

### Thread 2: Time Without Understanding
**Progression**: Gate rhythm → Seasonal cycles → Human puberty → Illness
**Question posed**: How does time pass for a being without temporal concepts?

### Thread 3: Loss Without Concept
**Progression**: Mother's scent fading → Sister's scent changing → Scent gone
**Question posed**: How is loss experienced bodily rather than conceptually?

### Thread 4: Earth Denied and Received
**Progression**: Burrow-longing → Hard ground → Hollow nest → Earth receiving body
**Question posed**: What is taken in captivity? What is returned in death?

### Thread 5: Freedom Glimpsed and Denied
**Progression**: Wire boundary → Brief escape → Reinforced walls
**Question posed**: What is the cost of tasting freedom only to have it taken away?

### Thread 6: Fierceness and Solitude
**Progression**: Kitten enters yard → Smaller sister drives it back repeatedly → Adult cat respects boundary → Larger sister dies → Cat enters hutch → Smaller sister cannot fight alone
**Question posed**: Does fierceness require something beyond oneself to protect?
