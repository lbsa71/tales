# Emotional Arc / Reader Experience Map

## Reader Journey Through "A Rabbit's Life"

**Note**: This emotional arc applies to both the novella (v1.2, 12,441 words) and short story (v2.0, 7,754 words) versions. The condensation tightened pacing but preserved all emotional peaks and narrative beats described below.

## Desired Reader Experience by Section

### Chapters 1-2: Wonder
**Reader feels**: Curiosity, sensory immersion
**Reader asks**: "How does a rabbit experience the world?"
**Emotional register**: Openness, slight disorientation, discovery

**Key moments**:
- First sensations of darkness and warmth
- Discovery of sister's body beside own
- First light, first sounds
- The pull toward earth that isn't there

### Chapters 3-4: Routine
**Reader feels**: Settling into rhythm, noticing patterns
**Reader asks**: "Is this all there is?"
**Emotional register**: Meditative calm with growing unease

**Key moments**:
- First contact with outdoor earth
- Hawthorn tree as central place
- Gate sounds becoming familiar
- Dominance established through eating order

### Chapter 5: Unfulfilled Instinct
**Reader feels**: Sharp emotional peak
**Reader asks**: "Why is this so devastating?"
**Emotional register**: Grief for something never born

**Key moments**:
- Pulling fur from belly
- Carrying hay to hollow
- Circling, arranging, waiting
- Nothing comes

*This scene should be the most emotionally intense moment of biological futility.*

### Chapters 6-7: Loss Beginning
**Reader feels**: Growing awareness, time accelerating
**Reader asks**: "How long has this been happening?"
**Emotional register**: Watching something end before it's named

**Key moments**:
- Human scent changes (puberty paralleling rabbit lives)
- Smaller sister's escapes and returns
- First scent of illness: iron, sweetness
- Belly swelling, breathing heavy

### Chapters 8-9: Death and Farewell
**Reader feels**: Stark confrontation with mortality
**Reader asks**: "What does loss feel like without understanding loss?"
**Emotional register**: Tender, clinical, elegiac

**Key moments**:
- Vet visits: antiseptic, metal, shaved fur
- Taken away, doesn't return
- Scent fading from straw and earth
- Body returned: cold, cloth-wrapped, no response
- Human weeping (sound without meaning)

### Chapter 10: Reckoning
**Reader feels**: Quiet transcendence, ethical confrontation
**Reader asks**: "What have we done to these beings?"
**Emotional register**: Serene devastation

**Key moments**:
- Survivor alone in hollow
- Wind through hawthorn sounds like sister
- Scent memories fading
- Final winter, body slowing
- Earth receiving what it was denied
- Facet closing, integration with all-being

## The Ethical Confrontation Architecture

The story should feel like a gradually tightening realization:

1. **"Oh, these are rabbits"**: Entering unfamiliar perspective
2. **"Oh, they experience the world so fully"**: Sensory immersion
3. **"Oh, they have instincts that go unfulfilled"**: Nesting scene
4. **"Oh, they can't understand their situation"**: Captivity unnamed
5. **"Oh, one is dying and can't comprehend medical help"**: Illness
6. **"Oh, the survivor experiences loss bodily"**: Scent fading
7. **"Oh, this entire life was constrained by human choice"**: Totality
8. **"Oh. What have we done. What responsibility do we bear."**: Reckoning

---

## Final Reader State

By story's end, readers should feel the full weight of the ethical question:

> "If I choose to take a sentient being from its mother, deny its instincts, control its entire existence, and end its life without it ever knowing freedom, what immense responsibility do I bear to make that life as rich as possible?"

The story doesn't moralize. It simply shows, with unflinching biological and sensory precision, what captivity is from the inside.

---

## Pacing Notes

- **Chapters 1-4**: Allow time for immersion, don't rush
- **Chapter 5**: Linger here, this is the emotional core
- **Chapters 6-7**: Compress time, show years passing
- **Chapters 8-9**: Slow down again for death
- **Chapter 10**: Gradual dissolution, sentence by sentence
