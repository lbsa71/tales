# THE LINE CATALOGUE
### *(franchise name TBD · working home: `older-than-truth`) · artifact 3 · the distinct lineages, the variation matrix, the proof a suite exists*

*This is not the law and it is not a story. The **Showrunner's Charter** (artifact 0) governs
this document; where they conflict, the charter wins. The **theme manifesto** (artifact 1) and
the **hidden iron-world** (artifact 2) sit above the individual lines and below the charter.
This catalogue does one job the others cannot: it holds **all the lineages in one frame at
once**, so that the rule the charter only states — §4.9, **no two lines solve merger the same
way** — can actually be **checked**. A manifesto can be obeyed one book at a time and still drift;
a catalogue makes drift visible, because every line is a row and you can see when two rows
rhyme.*

> **MAKER-ONLY.** This document names mechanism — the chord, the metabolism, the budding, the
> word that begins with *w*. **None of it is ever public-facing.** The reader/listener arrives
> at the iron-world by living inside its pattern (charter §4.2, §4.6); they never read it as a
> table. If a sentence from this catalogue could appear in a logline, a blurb, or a character's
> mouth, it has escaped its document. Keep it in.

---

## 0 · WHAT A "LINE" IS  *(the unit this catalogue catalogues)*

A **line** (a **lineage**) is the franchise's atom: **one merged collective, plus the one
inheritor it cannot absorb.** Both halves are required.

- It is **a merger** — a boundary dissolved, a *we* where there should be selves. This is the
  thing the Prime Directive (charter §1) is about: people who have stopped being *people to each
  other* and become one thing.
- It is **inherited** — it persists across generations and *hands something down.* The Prime
  Directive is stated as inheritance on purpose: *what is handed to you, and what is you.* A line
  is the machine that does the handing.
- It **produces an excluded one** — the inheritor who receives the legacy but cannot dissolve
  into the *we*: the protagonist, the only one in the bloodline who can love, *because love only
  happens across a gap and they are the only one who has one.*

**One line per book** (charter §4.8: every book is hermetically closed). Lines share the **deep
physics** of the hidden iron-world (artifact 2) and the **question** (artifact 1) — **never a
continuity, never a plot, never a cast.** A reader can start at any line. This catalogue is the
guarantee that when book two arrives it is a **different lineage**, not the first one repainted.

**Why a catalogue and not just a list:** the failure mode this franchise rolls toward is
**monster-of-the-week** (charter §6) — "another line, another victim, another reveal." The cure
is not taste; it is a matrix you must place a new line *on* before you may write it. If the new
row duplicates an existing row's answers on the mandatory axes (§1 below), it is the same book
again and must be rejected or reconfigured. **That rejection is this document's entire reason to
exist.**

## 1 · THE VARIATION MATRIX  *(the axes; this is where charter §4.9 is enforced)*

Every line is one row across these axes. Three of them are **MANDATORY-VARIATION** axes (charter
§4.9 names them explicitly): no two canon lines may match on **all three**. The rest are
**supporting** axes — they must be filled, they must be coherent, and they sharpen the distinction,
but the §4.9 firewall lives in the first three.

**The mandatory-variation axes (§4.9 — vary these or it is the same book):**

- **A · The dissolved boundary** — *what kind of merger is it, and by what mechanism does the
  boundary between selves disappear?* (Telepathic birth-link? Shared memory? One overwriting will?
  A dyad fused to a single self? Something colder?)
- **B · The excluded one** — *who is the inheritor who cannot merge, and what is the specific
  shape of their separateness?* (Genetic? Damage? An accident of birth order? A refusal?)
- **C · The shape of the consolation** — *what true, earned, un-free thing does the reader carry
  out, and in what gesture is it delivered?* (Run the knife test, charter §5: yes / yes / no.)

**The supporting axes (fill every one; they must cohere with A–C):**

- **D · The merged unit** — *who or what is the* we*?* (A matriline of women? A marriage? A town?
  A faith? A crew? A species?) Distinct from A: A is *how* they merge; D is *who* is merged.
- **E · The metabolism** — *what must the merger take from outside itself to persist, and what
  does that cost the world?* This is the line's draw on the hidden iron-world (artifact 2). It
  exists **only to generate moments** (charter §4.1, the generativity test); if a mechanism
  generates no scene worth illuminating, it does not exist.
- **F · The sincere antagonist** — *whose love, sincere from the inside, cannot perceive the
  other as real?* Must pass the villain test (charter §3, §4.3). Never malice.
- **G · The honest cost** — *who real pays, and is not redeemed by the ending?* The untied bow
  (charter §4.5). The price that keeps the consolation (C) from ringing hypocritical.
- **H · The buried human nerve** — *what ordinary, universal human thing does this supernatural
  configuration literalize?* If a reader can escape by thinking "my family / my world wasn't like
  that," this axis has failed the implication test (charter §3). The nerve is the un-exotic floor
  under the horror.
- **I · The register & restraint signature** — *medium, point of view, and how the hard material
  moves off-page* (charter §4.10). Not a §4.9 axis, but part of what makes a line *itself.*

A new line is **admissible** only if (1) it differs from every existing line on at least the
combination of A, B, and C; (2) D–I are all filled and cohere; (3) it passes the charter's
membership test (§7) and all six tests (§3). **Place it on the matrix before you write it.**

## 2 · LINE I — THE CHORD  *(Wren's lineage · the* SEEM *line · book one · the proof)*

> **Catalogue ID:** Line I. **Codename (maker-only):** *the Chord.* **Book:** *SEEM* (a season
> of 7 half-hour audio episodes). **Status:** manuscript complete; the proof-of-concept the whole
> charter is reverse-engineered from (charter §0). **Public-facing logline (lore-invisible):** *A
> rejected daughter of a family that shares one mind is sent to lure the men they need — until she
> lingers too long with the one man she was supposed to deliver, sees him too clearly, and her
> first act of mercy destroys the only people who were ever hers.*

**The essence in one breath:** a maternal bloodline is not a family of women but **one
consciousness that has worn the bodies of a matriline for generations**, to which being a
*separate person* is a fatal disease; the line's only inheritor who can love is **Wren**, the
daughter born *without* the link — and the play is Wren, years later, finally managing to
*perceive* the man she handed over, reconstructing the interior no one ever granted him.

Now the same line, axis by axis.

### A · The dissolved boundary  *(MANDATORY-VARIATION)*
A **matrilineal, birth-linked telepathic continuity** — *the chord.* Not a power, not a gift, not
a coven holding hands: **one self distributed across many skulls and many generations.** The
boundary between members was never crossed because it was **never built** — they are
*contiguous,* not *bonded;* their togetherness is **structural, not felt** (the cold under the
whole work). The "mother commanding the daughter" is one consciousness talking to itself across
two skulls. There is no seam in the *we* "because there is no seam in the we." In audio this is
rendered as **layered near-unison voices, close-mic'd, no room reverb** — a literal musical chord,
and (after the eldest's death) a chord **one voice short**, an audible ache that is never filled.

### B · The excluded one — WREN  *(MANDATORY-VARIATION)*
**Wren, the Failed Sister / the kept dud.** Her separateness is **genetic and exact:** every
budding must fire the bonding cascade to conceive, using the genes of an **outside father whose
attachment hardware is fully online.** Sometimes the dice fall *father* — and the child inherits
the **active bonding system instead of the link.** A being built to bond *across a gap to a
separate other* **cannot be merged**; she is **a human bridge born into an organism with no
acrosses.** So Wren is not noise in the system — she is **the line's own suppressed nature
resurfacing through the father's genes**, which keeps happening because they *must* keep firing
the cascade to breed at all.

Her exclusion is doubled: genetic by birth, **shame by rearing.** Raised inside the chord's
worldview with no outside vantage, she does not think her family is wrong — she thinks **she
failed the entrance exam.** The shame runs *inward.* She is an **unreliable narrator in one
direction only: she undervalues herself.** And — the cruelty worse than any cult survivor's —
**there is no "it was all a lie" exit, because the thing is real.** She really is missing the
sea the others swim in. Her redemption cannot be *the verdict was false;* it must be **the
verdict was correct, and the defect is the organ of love.** She must revalue the defect without
denying it.

### C · The shape of the consolation  *(MANDATORY-VARIATION — run the knife test)*
**The true thing:** *what you were taught is your brokenness — your inability to merge, to belong
easily, to stop feeling separate — is the exact organ that lets you love at all. The ones who
never feel alone are the ones who could only ever love themselves.* And its second half (the
mommy-issues capstone): *the instrument you were given for loving may be bent by the one who
raised you — and the bend does not disqualify the love.*

**The gesture that delivers it (never the thesis — charter §4.6):** the chord dies and is
**genuinely mourned** (the wholeness was real and beautiful); the Maiden falls out of the *we*
and is **caught, not merged — perceived as real for the first time, by the family's rejected
defect**; and two damaged sisters reach across the gap with four plain, unpractised words —
*"…I can see you too. From here."* — over a room with **no hum.** Then the world goes on.

- **Knife test (charter §5):** *True?* yes — kinship across a gap is real and load-bearing.
  *Earned?* yes — paid for in full by the cost (G). *Free?* **no** — it costs Wren the only thing
  she ever wanted (the merger) and costs her the man forever. **yes / yes / no.** On the edge.

### D · The merged unit
A **standing-wave triad: Maiden – Mother – Crone** (Liss – Vala – Edda). Crone = memory/dying;
Mother = power/doing; Maiden = becoming/next vessel. The organism **does not accumulate members** —
one-in / one-out. When the Crone dies the wave *advances* and **only then is one new Maiden
budded.** Therefore **one successful budding per generation — per death — not per man.** The line
is small, old, narrow (which is *why it survived being hunted* — there were never many). At
curtain-up the Crone has just died: the line is **amputated, grieving, metabolically destabilized,
hungry and hasty** — and grief is exactly the state in which one misjudges readiness.

### E · The metabolism  *(the draw on the hidden iron-world — render as hunger/residue, never stated)*
The chord cannot generate, from within, **the one thing it is made of the absence of: a self.** So
it must take **proximity to selfhood from outside,** through men a procurer brings home:
- **FEED** — most men are *meals:* drained of vitality and left **diminished**, "colours turned
  down" (the hollowed, "elf-shot" men of the folk-myth, rendered as physiological deficit, not a
  stolen soul).
- **TRAIN** — *reps:* men used so the Maiden can rehearse running the bonding cascade hot enough
  to function sexually **without letting it latch** into a bond. (This is why procuring is a
  career, not an event: you cannot rehearse not-falling-in-love without something to not-fall-in-
  love with.)
- **BREED** — the **budding,** once a generation: the real conception, through the present-tense
  man. **Not rape — perfected willful seduction (glamour):** the violation is **the use of his
  personhood without his knowledge** while he gives himself joyfully. *He makes love to a wall
  that is studying him.* Two women **envelop** him — one holds the spoken surface, one works the
  channel — and he feels one seamless field of fated wanting, *because there is no seam in the we.*
- **The ratchet (the decline):** every outside father reintroduces the active hardware;
  suppression gets **harder each generation;** the line has been **losing ground for a long
  time.** This generation — a kept dud who can love **and** a Maiden who catches the bond — is the
  **dam breaking.** So "the chord dies" is earned by **biology, not one bad night.**
- **Sons** (late, flat, never staged): a son can never be a node; his failure is known at birth;
  default fate is **immediate disposal as a non-person** — and **the discarded son and the
  harvested man are the same disposal.** The coldest confirmation of the inversion: the most
  loving family disposes of its infant sons as casually as a spent lover.

### F · The sincere antagonist — VALA, the Mother
**Never a villain — Lear, not a monster.** She believes the link *is* love; to her no boundary is
violated because they are *one* — and (the inversion) there is **no other there for her to
perceive.** Sincere devotion that has never once seen the child as separate. When the Maiden
individuates she feels **a limb dying** — she grieves *herself, extended,* not the daughter.
**She breaks the chord by needing it too badly:** grief over the dead Crone makes her **override
and rush the budding before the Maiden's suppression-training is complete,** which is the proximate
cause of the catastrophe. **Villain test:** her love is sincere from the inside — *pass.* The day
she becomes safe to hate, Line I has betrayed itself.

### G · The honest cost — DANIEL, the Man  *(do NOT tie his bow — charter §4.5)*
**Daniel: the living wound.** He is the only ordinary human in the work and **the most
human-feeling character** because of it. He is used, **hollowed, and walks back out into a world
that will never know what he was — never knowing himself what she felt.** No survival-as-victory,
no revenge, no reunion. Crucially, his is **the one seam-free thread in the work:** his feeling
for **Wren** is *never* preceded by a channel line — it is authentic, unmanufactured, and **she
can't perceive it; only the listener can.** She betrays him — hands the one real bond she ever
formed to the glamour — **never knowing she was handing over the single genuine connection either
of them had.** His justice is not rescue; it is the **whole play being Wren, too late,
reconstructing his interior** — *the form itself is the act of giving him back his personhood.*
**Bow test:** the story pays no justice its values cannot honestly pay — *untied.*

### H · The buried human nerve  *(the implication test — keep it un-exotic)*
**The enmeshing / narcissistic family whose "love" is extractive** — that consumes the member to
sustain itself, installs a voice in your head you mistake for your own, and feels your becoming-a-
separate-person as a *death* or a *betrayal.* Every supernatural fact literalizes one of these:
the birth-link = how parents unwittingly form children without consent; individuation-as-fatal-
disease = the vertigo of separating from an enmeshing parent (*is this me, or her, in me?*); the
fed-on, unfinished daughter = the child whose depletion was called "family" and who *thanked them
for it.* **If a listener can ever think "my family wasn't like THAT," the nerve is cut.**

### I · The register & restraint signature
**Audio literary-horror; retrospective first-person narrator; the three-stream device** (spoken
surface / cold telepathic channel / the man's installed interior) as the form's whole reason to
exist in sound — *you can hear a self being born.* Restraint is structural and maximal: the reps,
the feeding, the budding, the disposal of sons are all **off-mic, known by residue** (a flattened
voice, an empty place at the table, a household one note thin). **Consent-horror, not assault.**
**Never say "witch."** Render decline as **image and sound** (a fire to embers, then smoke), never
as physics. The dread *is* the listener arriving.

### Why Line I is canon  *(charter §7 membership test, ticked)*
1. Illuminates the Prime Directive from a real configuration — *merging vs. loving,* with the
   one who could never merge as the only one who can love. ✓
2. Hermetically closed — its own complete bible; no required reading order. ✓
3. Draws on the hidden iron-world (metabolism, the chord) but **never exposes it.** ✓
4. Obeys every non-negotiable (no villain; both griefs honoured; cost honest; arrival by
   recognition; restraint scaled up). ✓
5. Lands the knife — true / earned / un-free (C). ✓
6. Passes all six tests (generativity, implication, villain, knife, bow, arrival). ✓

## 4 · THE PROOF A SUITE EXISTS  *(why this is one configuration, not the only one)*

A catalogue with a single line proves nothing. The proof is **showing the configuration space is
large and Line I occupies one narrow point in it** — so that genuinely different lines are not
just permitted but *waiting.*

**The space, axis by axis — what Line I did NOT choose:**

- **A (dissolved boundary):** Line I chose *telepathy by birth.* The space also holds: a merger by
  **shared/overwriting memory** (you join by forgetting); by **one collective will or vow**
  (you join by consenting, and cannot leave); by a **dyad fused to a single self** (two, not many —
  a marriage, a twinship, a folie à deux); by **networked/technological sync;** by **appetite or
  contagion.** Each yields a different horror and a different excluded one.
- **B (the excluded one):** Line I chose *the genetically un-mergeable, raised in shame.* The space
  also holds: the one who **remembers when everyone else has forgotten;** the one who **refused the
  vow** and is kept as a servant, not a sister; the **late arrival** who joined too old to dissolve
  cleanly; the **child of the dyad** who can never enter the two-as-one that made them.
- **C (consolation shape):** Line I chose *kinship across the gap, received.* The space also holds:
  consolations of **memory kept faith with;** of **a refusal vindicated too late;** of **a single
  act of being-seen** with no relationship to follow — as long as each is **true, earned, and
  un-free** (charter §5).

**Two illustrative line-stubs** — *NOT CANON, NOT GREENLIT.* They exist **only to prove the matrix
is generative** (§4.9). A real Line II must be built into its own per-book bible (artifact 5) and
pass the membership test (charter §7); these are deliberately under-developed so no one mistakes a
proof for a plan.

> **Stub α — "the merged unit is a place, not a bloodline."**
> **A · boundary:** a small town / congregation merged by a **single shared memory** that quietly
> overwrites each newcomer's past until everyone remembers the same life. **B · excluded one:** the
> one resident whose memory **won't take** — who keeps their own past and is treated as ill, or
> ungrateful. **D · merged unit:** the town. **F · antagonist:** the keeper of the shared memory,
> who believes erasure is *mercy* (no one carries grief alone). **C · consolation:** that *to
> remember truly — even alone, even the pain — is to remain someone who can be met.* — Differs from
> Line I on **A, B, and C.** Admissible in principle.

> **Stub β — "the merged unit is two."**
> **A · boundary:** a marriage (or twinship) fused over decades into **one will with two mouths;**
> no telepathy, just total mutual prediction. **B · excluded one:** their **child**, who can never
> enter the two-as-one and grows up an audience to a love with no room in it. **D · merged unit:**
> the dyad. **F · antagonist:** the more-loving spouse, certain the marriage *is* the whole of love.
> **C · consolation:** that *the one shut out of the perfect dyad is the only one in the house who
> ever had to learn to actually see another person.* — Differs from Line I on **A, B, and C.**
> Admissible in principle.

**The anti-repetition checklist (run before approving any Line II):**
1. Does it match Line I on **A, B, *and* C**? If yes — it is *SEEM* in costume; **reject or
   reconfigure** (charter §4.9, §6 monster-of-the-week).
2. Is its antagonist sincere from the inside (F passes the villain test)? If no — fix.
3. Is its cost paid by someone real and left unredeemed (G; bow untied)? If no — untie it.
4. Does its consolation pass the knife (C: true / earned / un-free)? If no — it has fallen toward
   safe YA or hollow prestige (charter §5).
5. Can the reader escape via "my world wasn't like that" (H fails implication)? If yes — pull it
   back toward recognition.

If a proposed line clears all five, the suite has genuinely grown. If it cannot, the franchise was
about to write its first book twice.

## 5 · THE ONE-LINE ORDER OF OPERATIONS  *(for adding a line)*

*Find a merger Line I didn't make and an excluded one it didn't keep; give them a consolation the
knife approves; fill every axis; never say the mechanism; let no one be a villain; honour both
griefs; leave the cost unpaid; and place the row on the matrix before you write a word — if it
rhymes with the Chord on A, B, and C, it is not a new line, it is* SEEM *again.*

*Everything above is an elaboration of that sentence, and of the charter's, to which it answers.*
